<ABSTRACT>
This method comprises the following steps: a) forming a masking layer (28) on a substrate (26) from a resin sensitive to wavelength radiation less than 3000 A, such as polymethacrylate of methyl; (b) forming an intermediate mask (12) according to a desired configuration (17) from a phenol formaldehyde resin sensitized by a diazoic compound (may be formed either on a substrate or directly on the masking layer; case of the figure); c) transfer of the configuration (17) of the intermediate mask (12) to the masking layer (28) by exposure to radiation (32), at least some wavelengths are less than 3000 A; and (d) removal of the exposed parts (30) of the masking layer using a suitable solvent to form the mask. This process finds in particular application in the manufacture of masks used in the lithographic steps of the integrated semiconductor circuits.
