<ABSTRACT>
A composite material for operating temperatures is proposed up to 1250 ° C and 1350 ° C., which is chemically and mechanically stable at these temperatures, and mechanically stable, corrosive, gas-impermeable, and thermally well conductive, and at least on one side shows a low evaporation rate.The composite substance is constructed of at least two layers, one of which is consisting of a corrosion and high-temperature resistant nickel, iron or chromium alloy (high-temperature-resistant superalloy) and which is located on a highly warm refractory material.In addition, a method for producing such a composite is proposed, in which one connects the nickel, iron and chromium alloy and the refractory material by explosion plating with each other.
