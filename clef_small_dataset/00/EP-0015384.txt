<ABSTRACT>
In a method for producing red phosphor by thermal conversion of pushed gibeum phosphorus, the yellow phosphorus is continuously introduced into a locked ball mill preheated to a temperature of 280 to 590 ° C over its hollow shaft in such a quantity per time unit that in the KU Gel mill without further outer heat intake remains maintained the reactive temperature temperature. The amount of yellow phosphor, each time unit, is reduced at TEM performance level and increased at temperature drop. The entry of yellow phosphorus into the ball mill is terminated at a maximum of 50% of your filling volume. After cooling, the red phosphorus in the ball mill is subjected to overshoots with water of a wet grinding and finally removed as aqueous suspension. To carry out the method, a device can be used VER which consists of a bullet filled and rotatably mounted shafts (1, 2) drum (3), wherein at least one shaft is a hollow shaft and wherein on the hollow shaft (2) one Dosing device (10) is arranged for yellow phosphorus.
