<ABSTRACT>
Method for producing carbonic acid bisdipene molesters of carbonate-increasing polyalkylene oxide diols and their use for the preparation of high molecular weight, segmented thermoplastically processable polyether polycarbonates.Polyalkylene oxide diols are heated with carbon acid bisaryl esters in vacuo at temperatures between 100 and 200 ° C wherein less than 1 mole of carbonic acid bis-aryl ester is used per OH group of the polyalkylene oxide diol.Subsequently, diphenols are added.The resulting reaction product is reacted with other diphenols and with phosgene at pH values between 9 and 14 and temperatures between 0 ° and 80 ° C to polyether polycarbonates.
</ABSTRACT>
