<ABSTRACT>
brazing of metal parts employing a thin, homogeneous,  ductile, filler metal foil is disclosed.  the brazing foil, useful for  brazing stainless steels, is not in excess of . 0025 inch (3. 81 x  10⁻³ cm) thick and has a composition consisting essentially of  0 to about 4 atom percent iron, 0 to about 21 atom percent  chromium, 0 to about 19 atom percent boron, 0 to about 12  atom percent silicon, 0 to about 22 atom percent phosphorus  and the balance nickel and incidental impurities.  in addition to  containing the foregoing elements within the above-noted  composition ranges, the composition must be such that the  total of iron, chromium and nickel ranges from about 76 to 84  atom percent and the total or boron, silicon and phosphorus  ranges from about 16 to 24 atom percent the ductile foil  permits fabrication of preforms of complex shapes which do  not require binders and/or fluxes necessary for brazing pow­ ders presently used to braze stainless steels and nickel base  alloys.
