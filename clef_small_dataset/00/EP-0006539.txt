<ABSTRACT>
a novel one-step process for the preparation of mandelic acid esters of the general formula <image>  in which    r represents alkyl,   x represents alkyl, aryl, hydroxyl, alkoxy, halogen, haloalkyl and/or nitro, and    n represents an integer from 0 to 3, by reaction of mandelonitriles (benzaldehyde cyanohydrins), alcohols and hydrogen chloride, in which, if desired, the nitrile nitrogen can be simultaneously obtained in the form of solid ammonium chloride.  the process is characterised in that the corresponding mandelonitriles are reacted, per mol, with 3 to 20 mol of an alcohol of the formula r-oh and either with 1. 8 to 3 mol of hydrogen chloride or with the addition of up to 1 mol of water containing 1. 2 to 2. 4 mol of hydrogen chloride at temperatures between 40 and 160 deg c and after a reaction time of about 3 to 10 hours  a) worked up in a customary manner and the products isolated or b) in the case in which it is desired to obtain the nitrile nitrogen in the form of solid ammonium chloride, excess hydrogen chloride is neutralised with gaseous ammonia, an aromatic, optionally chlorinated hydrocarbon is added and, for working up, the excess alcohol is removed by distillation, the precipitated crystalline ammonium chloride is filtered off after cooling and the mandelic acid ester is isolated from the organic filtrate by distillation.  the mandelic acid esters are used as organic intermediates, for example for the preparation of herbicidal active substances.
