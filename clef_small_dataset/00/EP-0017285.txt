<ABSTRACT>
The present invention relates to the recovery of moryden, vanadium, aluminum as well as cobalt and nickel from residues of hydrodesul catalysts.According to the invention, the so-called hydrocarbon hydrocarbon catalyst residues are subjected to a chlorination by means of a mixture of gaseous chlorine, hydrochloric acid and water vapor under conditions such as molybdenum and the molybdenum.Vanadium are volatilized in the form of oxychlorides and aluminum in the form of chloride and the products thus volatilized by means of an aqueous medium containing hydrochloric acid.The vapor content of the gaseous chlorination mixture does not exceed, about 15% by volume relative to the total gaseous mixture.
