<ABSTRACT>
a video extractor for use in a pulse radar apparatus  comprises a shift-register memory (1) for storing a number of  words, each of which contains information about the position  and size of a target as established for each of the range quanta  in which the desired range interval is divided.  the video  extractor contains a range counter (4), whose counting rate is  equal to the rate at which said words are shifted into the  memory.  an arithmetic unit (2) receives the words shifted out  of the memory and, in response to the video and angular data  from the pulse radar apparatus and to the range data provided  by the range counter, updates as necessary all information  contained in said words with each successive range sweep.   because all information referring to one target has to be  concentrated in a single word the arithmetic unit transfers the  updated information to following or preceding words drawn  from the memory.  a readout circuit (7) connected to the  arithmetic unit and to the range counter is used to read out the  information referring to the particular target.
