method for preparing a high-activity supported nickel catalyst, and a catalyst thereby prepared.

<ABSTRACT>
a method for preparing high activity supported nickel  catalysts comprises depositing nickel nitrate on a support  (which is either an inorganic refractory oxide, e. g.  alumina or  silica-alumina, or carbon), heating the resulting composite in  a flowing gaseous atmosphere to a temperature either in the  range of from 100-270°c when the atmosphere is substantially  free of molecular hydrogen, or in the range 100-220°c  when the atmosphere contains molecular hydrogen, avoiding  temperature excursions above the stated maxima.  the  heating decomposes nickel nitrate to the oxide and the  resulting supported nickel oxide is then reduced to nickel in a  reducing atmosphere at a temperature in the range 230-400°c  to produce the high activity supported nickel catalyst.  the hydrogenation activity of the catalyst is considerably  higher than that of prior supported nickel catalysts  having the same nickel surface area per unit weight.  
</ABSTRACT>
