process for the preparation of esters of butanedicarboxylic acid.

<ABSTRACT>
1.  a process for the preparation of butanedicarboxylic acid esters by : a) reacting butadiene or hydrocarbon mixtures containing butadiene with carbon monoxide and a c1 - to c4 -alkanol in the presence of a tertiary nitrogen base and a cobalt carbonyl catalyst at from 80 degrees to 150 degrees c under superatmospheric pressure, b) removing the greater part of the tertiary nitrogen base together with any excess hydrocarbon, and c) reacting the resulting pentenoic acid ester, in the presence of the catalyst remaining in the reaction mixture, and in the presence of the remaining amount of tertiary nitrogen base, with carbon monoxide and a c1 - to c4 -alkanol at from 140 degrees to 200 degrees c under superatmospheric pressure, to give the butanedicarboxylic aicd ester, characteized in that, in stage c), at least 50 % of the cobalt catalyst in not in the form of a pi-allyl complex with butadiene. 
</ABSTRACT>
