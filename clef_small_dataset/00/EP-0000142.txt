<ABSTRACT>
Preparation of polyamides by heating alkandicarboxylic acids with 6 to 12 carbon atoms and diamines of the formula NH₂NNH₂, in which R is an alkylene residue having 6 to 12 carbon atoms or the radicals refers to polyamide-forming temperatures under elevated pressure and removal of water,wherein from 75 to 90 weight percent aqueous solutions of salts from dicarboxylic acids and diamines which are obtained by neutralization of less concentrated salt solutions containing a corresponding amount of the respective dicarboxylic acid in excess dissolved with the corresponding diamines.
