<ABSTRACT>
In a process for producing highly integrated semiconductor assemblies with adjacent, highly doped semiconductor zones (32, 34), opposite line type is ensured that in overlap ranges of these two semiconductor zones, the formation of crystal errors and their consequences are avoided.Crystal error can be avoided when the first N-conducting semiconductor zone (32) by doping with arsenic in a maximum concentration in the range of 5 x 10²⁰ to 2 x 10²¹¹ atoms / cm³ and the second, the first at least partially overlapping P-conductingSemiconductor zone (34) is made by doping with boron at a maximum concentration in the range of 5 x 10¹⁹ to 3 x 10²⁰ atoms / cm³.
