<ABSTRACT>
Method for continuous production of finely divided, water-insoluble, still-bound water-containing sodium aluminum silicates of the general formula: x Na₂O. Al₂O₃. Y SiO₂ (x = 0.2 - 1.5; y = 1.5 - 10) by mixing water-resolved sodium aluminate and water-resolved sodium silicate in the presence of excess sodium hydroxide solution. The aluminate solution to be used corresponds to a molar composition 1 Al₂O₃: 1 - 10 Na₂O: 20 - 200 H₂O, the silicate solution of a molar composition Na₂O: SiO₂ = 0.1 - 10 and H₂O: SiO₂ = 5 - 200. The solutions are correspondingly in a weight ratio a computational total composition with respect to the molar ratios of 1.5 - 15 Na₂O: 1 Al₂O₃: 1 - 15 SiO₂: 50 - 400 H₂O at 0 - 103 ° C using a nozzle system having at least two liquid channels in which the aluminate and the silicate solution separated The nozzle outlet opening is supplied, sprayed together in such a way that the two solutions occur until after leaving the outlet opening and react with each other in the spray jet to form an X-ray amorphic sodium aluminum silicate gel. Preferably, corresponding gels to zeolithic cation-exchanging sodium aluminum silicates of type Naa, Nax and P are crystallized.
</ABSTRACT>
