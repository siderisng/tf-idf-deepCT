<ABSTRACT>
a nonwoven fabric is prepared by simultaneously heat­ ing and compressing spaced, discrete areas of a nonwoven,  bondable fiber web containing an attenuating bonding liquid  in excess of the minimum amount (the "peak bonding quan­ tity") required to produce maximum fabric strength.  the  attenuating bonding liquid is a bonding liquid which, if used in  quantities exceeding the peak bonding quantity by no more  than 400% of the web weight (prior to addition of bonding  liquid), provides a nonwoven fabric having an average bend­ ing modulus at least 20% lower than that of a fabric prepared  using the peak bonding quantity of liquid.
