<ABSTRACT>
1.  an improved process for the preparation of a carboxylic acid ester of beta-formyl-crotyl alcohol by rearrangement of the corresponding carboxylic acid ester of 2-formyl-2-hydroxy-but-3-ene or of an acetal or acylate thereof in the presence of copper or a copper compound at from 50 to 250 degrees c followed, where relevant, by hydrolytic cleavage of the acetal or acylate group, characterized in that a) the rearrangement is carried out in the presence of copper (i) chloride as the catalyst, and b) the low-boiling by-products formed during the rearrangement are continuously removed from the reaction mixture in a conventional manner. 
</ABSTRACT>
