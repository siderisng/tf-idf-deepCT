<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to an electrochemical sensor of the relative concentrations in a fluid mixture, of the type using a measuring cell constituted by two electrodes (E₁, E₂) placed on either side of a solid electrolyte (E₁)..According to the invention, the measuring cell, a planar structure, comprises an electrode (E₂) made of prolonged catalytic material according to at least one direction parallel to the plane of the cell, on a predominated length terminated (LC).The fluid mixture to be analyzed is brought to the thermodynamic equilibrium by contacting this zone (1) of the electrode (E₂) or by diffusion within it, before reaching a second zone (2) of theElectrode (E₂) or measurement area.Application to the regulation of combustion engines and boiler burning.
