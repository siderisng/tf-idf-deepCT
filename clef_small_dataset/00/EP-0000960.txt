<ABSTRACT>
A device for switching steps is proposed in motor vehicles in which undesirable high-circuit operations are suppressed, which are then triggered in conventional automatic transmissions when the accelerator pedal is withdrawn. Such a high-circuitry is then undesirable when the vehicle is travels a gradient route, inserting or rolling into a curve. According to the invention, a high switching operation is suppressed when the accelerator pedal is returned jerkily. In one embodiment of the invention, for this purpose, the pressure medium flow to a driving pedal actuated control slide is influenced by a membrane switch coupled to the intake pipe; In a second embodiment, a voltage corresponding to the accelerator pedal position is electronically differentiated and, depending on the load signal coming from the accelerator pedal, is locked at the input of an electronic transmission control stage; In a third embodiment, finally, when detecting a high switching state due to load reduction, the accelerator pedal position is determined at predetermined intervals and suppresses at zero load of the highshift.
