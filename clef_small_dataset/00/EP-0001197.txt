<ABSTRACT>
The coupling products of the cytidine-diphospho choline with amino compounds are obtained by the cede case which consists: to oxidize the cytidine-diphosphoshellin, to attach the dialdehyde ebutaneous by the preceding oxidation of the ribose of the cytidine on the groups NH₂ The amino solution in alkaline aqueous solution, to stabilize the coupling thus produced by reduction and to recover the molecular macro product thus obtained in the form of an aqueous solution, which can be lyophilized to provide a powder adapted to the conservation. The coupling is made between oxidized ribose groups of cytidine-diphosphocholine and the lysine residues of amino compounds. The products of the invention are suitable for vaccines against parasites belonging to those with molecules related to a phosphorylcholine residue, such as intestinal or respiratory parasites. They are also suitable for the determination of biological compounds reacting with phosphorycholine.
