<ABSTRACT>
a method of preparing microfibrous tricalcium silicate  makes use of relatively cheap starting material, namely  crystalline silica.  a suspension of crystalline silica, preferably  quartz, in an aqueous suspension of calcium hydroxide is  heated above the normal boiling point, the molar ratio  ca(oh)₂:sio₂ in the system being not less than 2. 5:1.  the  fibres produced have a high aspect ratio, and are useful as a  substitute for asbestos.
