<ABSTRACT>
The device for determining the mechanical rear frequency of blood vessels, tendons, bones or similar organs in bodies (15) consists of a donor (7.8) for mechanical vibrations of variable frequency and a vibration sensor (11). The encoder (7,8) for mechanical vibrations has transmission means for transmitting the mechanical vibrations from the encoder (7.8) to the body (15) containing the organ to be examined. By means of a second transfer agent (4), the vibrations aroused in the organ are transferred from the surface of the body (15) to the oscillation sensor (11). Both transfer means (3,4) are mechanically decoupled within a support tube (2), which can be placed on the body (15). They have a defined distance (D) on the mouth of the support tube (2). At its other end, the support tube (2) is rigidly connected to a frame (1) in which the encoder (7,8) for mechanical vibrations and the vibration sensor (11) are mutually decoupled.
