<ABSTRACT>
a process for producing a latent heat-bulkable  polyethylene terephthalate yarn comprising melt spinning a  polyethylene terephthalate fiber-forming polymer into a plur­ ality of filaments (10), cooling the melt-spun filaments in  quench zone (14) below the second order transition tempera­ ture and taking up the filaments as a yarn at a speed in excess  of 8000 feet per minute, characterised in that the cooled melt-­ spun filaments are divided into at least two groups and at least  one of the groups is subjected to a heat treatment above the  second order transition temperature, the filaments then being  recombined.  
</ABSTRACT>
