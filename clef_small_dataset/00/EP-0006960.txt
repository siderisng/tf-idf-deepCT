<ABSTRACT>
the coordinate input device of the invention includes an  input plate (100) comprising two light-guiding path arrays  formed by parallel arrays of a required number of light-­ guiding paths (11, 12) which include light-scattering bodies  (22), the two light-guiding path arrays being superposed such  that the parallel light-guiding paths in one array perpendicu­ larly cross the parallel light-guiding paths in the other.  when a  point on the input plate (100) is irradiated by means of a light  pen (10), the light is scattered by the light-scattering body (22)  located in the light-scattering paths (11, 12) perpendicularly  intersecting at the irradiated point.  the light is transmitted to  the end faces (11a, 12a) of these light-guiding paths where it is  detected.  the corresponding detection signal is then used to  obtain the positional coordinates of the light pen (10) expre­ ssed in terms of a two-value system.  
</ABSTRACT>
