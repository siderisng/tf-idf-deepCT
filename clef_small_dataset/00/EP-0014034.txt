<ABSTRACT>
a rotary valve characterised in that it includes a holl­ ow open ended cylinder (3) having a top (5) end and a  bottom (6) end; at least one radlally projecting axially ex­ tending blade (11) in the cylinder dividing the latter into a  plurality of axially extending open ended compartments,  each blade spirraling from the top end of the cylinder to  the bottom end thereof so that the top end of a compart­ ment is circumferentially off-set from the bottom end there  of; a top and a bottom plate each respectively in slidable  sealing engagement with the top and bottom ends of the  compartments, the plates and the cylinder being rotatable  relative to one another; an inlet (8) in the top plate; and an  outlet (9) in the bottom plate, the inlet and outlet being so  disposed relative to each other that while the top end of a  particular compartment communicates with the inlet, the  bottom end of that compartment does not communicate  with the outlet.
