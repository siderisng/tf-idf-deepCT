<ABSTRACT>
A method of flux-free brazing or welding of aluminum parts is given. The Be network-resistant gas types, such as O₂ and H₂O, which inhibit the wetting, are controlled by rapid heating (min destinities 100 ° C / min) within a critical temperature range, such as 400 to 590 ° C, while heating up to hardening temperatures. The formation of the porosity within the oxide film and the collection of promoter and wetting agents at the interface with the oxide film takes place simultaneously and excludes an interruption of the wetting effect by gaseous O₂ or H₂O. The wicked sucking of the benet dividing means by the film porosity within the critical temperature range then occurs due to the bounded, for the reaction of gaseous O₂ or H₂O with the film for forming a double oxide transferable time. The heating can be carried out in a vacuum of 10⁻³ Torr or less or in an inert atmosphere under pulsating heating, predetermined that the critical heating rate is maintained within half of this critical temperature range.
