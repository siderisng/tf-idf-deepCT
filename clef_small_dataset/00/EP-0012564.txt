<ABSTRACT>
a thermal conducting path between a relatively movable  heat source (24, 20, 14) and a relatively fixed heat sink ele­ ment (6, 15), is defined as a relatively narrow non-contacting  conducting gap (10) between opposing conducting surfaces  (12, 8).  in an embodiment where the heat source (24, 20, 14) is  gimbal mounted, for movement with respect to two ortho­ gonal axes (p, y), its conductive surface (12) has a convex  partial spherical shape while the relatively fixed surface (8) of  the heat sink element (6, 15) has a concave partial spherical  shape.  in order to maintain a constant gap (10), the opposing  surfaces have a common spherical centre located at the  crossover (30) of said orthogonal axes (p, y) and the spheri­ cal radii dimensions (r₁, r₂) differ by the distance of said rela­ tively narrow non-contacting gap (10) there-between.
