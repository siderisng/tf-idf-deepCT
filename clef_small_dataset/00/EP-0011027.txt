<ABSTRACT>
A method of regenerating an adsorbent material in the form of particles charged with at least two volatile compounds of different thermal stabilities.Calcination of one of the volatile compounds is caused in a first contact column (8), wherein the material is caused and suspended by an oxidizing gas stream, therein at a high temperature.The desorbed gaseous products in this first column are resetsorbed by cooled in a second contact column (20).Main applications: regeneration of alumina particles having adsorbed tar and fluorinated compounds.
