<ABSTRACT>
A method for producing trans-4-alkyl-cyanoaryl cyclohexanes of the general formula in the R is an unbranched or once branched aliphatic hydrocarbon radical with up to 12 carbon atoms and N has zero, 1 or 2. Based on a corresponding arylcarboxylic acid of the allegemeine formula, this is initially hydrogenated a birch reduction and then after esterification in the presence of Pd / activated carbon to a mixture of the two CIS trans-isomers cyclohexyl-4-carboxylic acid ethyl esters. By subsequently heating the ester mixture under absolutely anhydrous conditions in ethanol in the presence of sodium and under inert gas, the CIS ester present in the isomer mixture is quantified quantitatively in the corresponding trans ester. The resulting stereochemically pure trans ester is then sampled stereospecific. In the last step, the cyano group is introduced. The obtained compounds of general formula I are particularly suitable as components of liquid crystal mixtures.
