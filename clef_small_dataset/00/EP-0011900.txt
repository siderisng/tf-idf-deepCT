<ABSTRACT>
process for the conversion of dimethyl ether into a mix­ ture of substantially aliphatic hydrocarbons and water by  means of a catalyst containing boron.  the dimethyl ether is  put in contact, at a temperature of over 250 °c, with a catalyst  that consists of a crystalline modification of silica in whose  crystal lattice a number of silicon atoms have been replaced  by boron atoms.  the amount of boron in the crystalline modification,  expressed as the molar ratio of the oxides b₂o₃, and sio₂, may  vary between 0. 0005 and 0. 025.  
</ABSTRACT>
