<ABSTRACT>
a diluted whole blood sample is hydrodynamically  focused to a given point.  a light beam is transversely  focused on the same point and the principles of dark  field microscopy are utilized, detecting scattered light  and thereby discriminating platelets from red blood cells  based on cell volume and refractivity.
