absorbent papers and a process for their production.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
an absorbent paper is formed from 5-95% by  weight of insoluble amino-formaldehyde resin fibres of  1-10 mm length and 1-30 µm diameter and correspond­ ingly 95-5% cellulose pulp.  where the canadian stand­ ard freeness of the cellulose pulp is below 310 x + 140  ml.  (where x is the proportion of lignin free pulp in the  cellulose pulp) the proportion of amino-formaldehyde  fibres is such that the blend has a freeness above 220 x  + 400 ml.  the paper may be made by forming an aqueous  slurry of the fibrous constituents into sheet form followed  by dewatering.
