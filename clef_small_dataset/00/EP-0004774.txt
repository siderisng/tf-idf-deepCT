<ABSTRACT>
a process for preparing a cationic starch dispersion  wherein an alkaline, gelatinized starch is reacted with a  cationic reagent (e. g.  a tertiary amine salt) either concurrently  with, or subsequently to, the cooking (gelling) process  resulting in a product in which the cationic reagent is  chemically bound to the starch molecule.  the process is  conducive to the on-site preparation of the cationic starch  and can be utilized without additional treatment as a  papermaking adjunct, textile size, waste-water treatment or  tailings flocculent.
