<ABSTRACT>
olefins are hydroformylated using carbon monoxide and hydrogen at 10 to 325 bar and 100 to 170 deg c in the presence of cobalt carbonyl or rhodium carbonyl complexes to give the corresponding, one carbon atom longer, aldehydes and alcohols using perpendicular cylindrical reactors, in which a coaxial cylindrical construction provides for forced circulation of the reaction material, by withdrawing the reaction material in the lower part of the reactor.  <image>
