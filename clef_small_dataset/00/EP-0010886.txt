<ABSTRACT>
a process for the dimerisation of acrylonitrile to di­ cyanobutene and methyleneglutaronitrile under the influen­ ce of a phosphinite or phosphonite catalyst of the general  formula    wherein at least r₂ and optionally also r₁ and/or r₃ are  electron-donating substituents (the other significations of  r₁ and r₃ being given in the description) and r is alkyl or  cycloalkyl and m and n are each 1 or 2, provided that (m  + n) equals 3, e. g.  isopropyl bis-3,4-dimethylphenylphos­ phinite, uses as solvent a mixture of a proton-donating or­ ganic solvent (e. g.  isopropanol), an aromatic hydrocarbon  solvent (e. g.  toluene) and an aliphatic hydrocarbon solvent  (e. g.  methylcyclohexane), e. g.  in the ratio of aromatic hy­ drocarbon : aliphatic hydrocarbon : proton donating sol­ vent : acrylonitrile of 5 : 5 : 1 : 3, so facilitating the isola­ tion of the products by phase separation or liquid/liquid  extraction.  
</ABSTRACT>
