<ABSTRACT>
The electrochemical catalytic system consists of: - an anode comprising an oxidizable metal, a cathode comprising a compound selected from the plate, iron and the vitreous carbon, an electrolyte comprising on the one hand a compound selected from a dinitrosylfer chloride. and a mixture of iron chloride and nitrogen monoxide no, and the other an electrochemical solvent. The selective dimerization method of cyclohexenic-shaped dimming diolefins consists in using the catalytic system above by putting in the presence in an electrochemical cell comprising an anode, a cathode and a reference electrode: a suitable solvent, the diolefin to dimerize , and a compound selected from dinitrosylfer chloride and the mixture of iron chloride and nitrogen monoxide no, then carrying the cathode, with respect to the reference electrode, to a potential corresponding to the last wave of reduction said compound and maintaining this potential for the duration of the reaction (preferably using a potentiostat that automatically provides the voltage and current between anode and cathode). The anode is aluminum or iron; FECL₂ or FECL₃ can be used in association with NO; The solvent is preferably propylene carbonate. This method makes it possible to prepare with a selectivity of 100% of the dimetres with cyclohexenic structure from conjugated diolefins.
