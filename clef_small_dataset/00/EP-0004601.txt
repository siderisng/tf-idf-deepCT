process for removing highly odorous components from particulate olefine polymers.

<ABSTRACT>
a process for removing high-odour constituents from polyolefin powders involves the action of steam in the absence of air, with the steam treatment lasting from 0. 5 to 15 hours, preferably from 1 to 10 hours, the amount of steam being from 5 to 50% by weight, preferably from 5 to 30% by weight, based on the weight of the polyolefin, and the steam pressure being from 0. 1 to 10 bar, preferably 1 bar.  this process allows complete removal of odorous substances from the polyolefin without the removed substances polluting the environment. 
</ABSTRACT>
