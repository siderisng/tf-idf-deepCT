<ABSTRACT>
Process for the preparation of amorphous crystallizable sodium aluminum silicates by continuously mixing a sodium aluminate with a na triumseilicate solution in a stepy-acting mixing distance, wherein the two solutions to be mixed a computer composition with respect to the moles ratios of 1.5 to 9 Na₂O: 1 Al₂O₃: 1 to 7 SiO₂: 40 to 400 H₂O. In this case, one of the two reaction components is divided into a plurality of partial streams, one of which one of which the total current of the other reaction components immediately continuously inflicted (1), the further because it is only downstream of the current of the formed reactive mixture is also continuously metered (2, 3, 4) . As a step-like mixing distance, a pipe reactor, which has a ratio of pipe length to pipe through knife of more than 500, is used. In a corresponding overall composition of the two solutions to be mixed, the ge formed amorphous sodium aluminum silicate can then be added to a zeolite molecular sieve of the type Naa crystalline.
