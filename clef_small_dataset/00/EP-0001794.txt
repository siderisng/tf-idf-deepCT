<ABSTRACT>
Method for producing a grounded semiconductor wafer for the production of integrated circuits.On the back of the semiconductor wafer, a damaged layer is made up to a depth of about 8 to 35 μm, then the semiconductor wafer is heated to a temperature of about 800 to 1150 ° C for about 1 to 3 hours, and then theSemiconductor disc quickly cooled to a temperature below about 600 ° C.Thereafter, both sides of the semiconductor disk will be polished to form a polished, substantially faultless surface on the front side and a smooth surface on the back so that a residual layer remains with crystallographic damage supplies an additional gardening during the manufacture of the integrated circuits.
