<ABSTRACT>
A circuit for reloading the output node (a) of a field effect transistor circuit includes a field effect transistor (1) which is made periodically conductive via a capacitor (C1). As a result, the capacitance (CE) present at the output node (a) of the field effect transistor circuit is periodically redundant over the positive pole (VH) of the supply voltage. The first-time charging of this capacity (CE) is carried out by a powerful pulse of the field effect transistor circuit which is a drive circuit (TR). In order to prevent current flow through the field effect transistor circuit by the field effect transistor circuit at a low output potential of the field effect transistor circuit, and thus the generation of a power loss, the field effect transistor (2) is provided. Its gate electrode is connected to the positive pole (VH) of the supply voltage. The field effect transistor (2) is therefore conductive at low potential at the output node (A) and transmits this potential to the gate electrode of the field effect transistor (1). This does not conduct because its gate-source voltage is smaller than the threshold voltage.
