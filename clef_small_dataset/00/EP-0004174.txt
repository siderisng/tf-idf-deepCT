<ABSTRACT>
a process for refining a metal by separating at least a  portion of liquid from a solid-liquid composition formed  from the metal comprises heating the metal in a crucible (30)  from the non-equilibrium solidus temperature while forcing  the liquid under pressure through a filter (32).  in the case of  dendritic alloys, dendrites in the solid are converted to a  fine-grain structure.  a homogeneous fine grained metal alloy workpiece can  be produced free of second phases, possessing at least 80%  of the theoretical maximum equilibrium solute content, and  having a thickness of at least 3 mm.  
</ABSTRACT>
