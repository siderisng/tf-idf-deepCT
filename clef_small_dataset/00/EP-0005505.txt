open-cell foamed polyurethane resins containing compounds forming chelates and/or complexes of chelates, process for their preparation and their application to the adsorption of heavy-metal ions from waste water

<ABSTRACT>
open-cell foamed polyurethane resins, a process for their preparation, and their use, comprising organic polyisocyanates, polyols, blowing agents, catalysts and, if desired, additives, and containing bonded chelating agents (a) which contain no nco group-reactive hydrogen atoms, and/or contain incorporated chelate complexes (b) having stability constants log k < 5, with the proviso that the chelating agents (a) or chelate complexes (b) form, with copper ions, 1 : 1 chelates having stability constants log k >/= 5.  suitable chelating agents (a) or chelate complexes (b) are, in particular, heterocyclic aromatic amines,  beta -diketones and salts of aminocarboxylic acids.  the open-cell foamed polyurethane resins are suitable for adsorption of heavy-metal ions from effluent possibly containing organic solvents. 
</ABSTRACT>
