<ABSTRACT>
In a process for continuously converting Ne metal sulfide concentrates in an elongated, lying reactor, sulfidic concentrates of copper, nickel antimony, cobalt, lead and surcharges are charged over a considerable length in controlled amounts on the melt, oxidizing gases in regulated amounts with protective media of blown down into the oxidation zone and reducing gases into the reduction zone, withdrawn a ne-metal-rich phase and a non-metal sleeve phase on the opposite end of the reactor, which passes in countercurrent and substantially continuous, layered currents and the gradient of oxygen activity in the melt from a maximum at the metal end to a minimum of slag end. For lowering the metal content of the slag phase and improving the heat utilization as well as enabling auxiliary heating, the gas atmosphere is guided in countercurrent to the slag phase and the exhaust gas is withdrawn at the outlet end of the ne-metal-rich phase.
