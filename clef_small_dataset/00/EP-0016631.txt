<TITLE>
removal of hydrogen sulphide and carbonyl sulphide from gas stream.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>

<ABSTRACT>
hydrogen sulphide and carbonyl sulphide are removed  from a gas stream obtained by the gasification of coal or  heavy hydrocarbon and containing inter alia hydrogen,  carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, hydrogen sulphide and  carbonyl sulphide by a process which comprises washing  the gas stream with an absorbent in a first absorbtion stage  (100) to remove almost all of the hydrogen sulphide in said  gas stream; reacting the gas leaving the first absorbtion  stage (100) with water vapour in a shift converter system (15)  utilizing a sulphur resistant shift catalyst to convert at least  part of the carbon monoxide into hydrogen and to hydrolyze  the carbonyl sulphide to hydrogen sulphide; and washing  the remaining gas with absorbent in a second absorbtion  stage (24) to remove substantially all of the hydrogen  sulphide remaining.  
</ABSTRACT>
