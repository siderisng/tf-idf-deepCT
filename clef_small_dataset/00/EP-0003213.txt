<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to an optoelectronic sensor with at least one sensor element according to the principle of charge injection (CID), wherein the surface of a doped semiconductor body 1 is two tightly adjacent, against each other, and relative to the semiconductor surface by a thin insulating layer 5, via separate control lines controllable electrodes 4, 10 face. According to the invention according to the semiconductor body 1 on its surface under the one electrode 10 is a more doped region 6 of the type of semiconductor doping, which extends slightly into the semiconductor surface lying under the other electrode 4. As a result, between the two electro, z. b. a row electrode and a column electrode, a narrow potential barrier generates. If the charge which is optically generated under an electrode is shifted under the other electro DE, this potential barrier prevents a flow through the charge under the emptied electrode. This allows a floating reading out, in which coupling of the line lines can eliminate the column lines. Preferably, the more doped region tapers wedge-shaped below the other electrode 4 and is generated by implantation, using the field oxide and the electrode 4 as the implantation mask, the edges of which are chamfered.
