light source unit for an endoscope.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
a light source unit for an endoscope is provided with a  number of flash tubes (28, 30, 32, 34) which are caused in turn  to emit a light.  on a optical path (14) passing through a light-in­ cident surface of a light guide (2) of the endoscope there are  arranged, in corresponding relationship to the flash tubes, a  number of plane reflecting mirrors (8, 10, 12, 14) which reflect  the lights from the flash tubes toward the light-incident surface  (3) of the light guide, respectively.  the reflecting mirrors are  each movable in parallel by a rotary solenoid (22, 24, 26).  this  solenoid operates at the time when the light emission from one  of the flash tubes is completed.  thus, the solenoid, for the pur­ pose of giving the optical path for a succeeding one of the  flash tube, removes from that optical path the reflecting mirror  corresponding to said one of the flash discharge tube.  
</ABSTRACT>
