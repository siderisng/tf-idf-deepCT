<ABSTRACT>
process for the continuous recovery of solid polycarbonate  from its solutions in organic solvents, consisting in:   a) subdividing the polycarbonate solution by steam (water  vapor) at either sonic or ultra-sonic speed by means of a de  laval type nozzle;   b) conveying the dispersion thus obtained into a tubular  heat exchanger having an increasing cross-section, preferably  spirally-shaped or with elbow strethes having a thermal  exchange coefficient of 500-1500 kcal/h. m² °c, in which the  heat dispersion is kept at 100°-140°c for periods of time comprised  between 0. 05 and 5 seconds;   c) separating the solid from the vapors in a cyclon;   d) drying of the solid in a water vapor fluid bed.  
</ABSTRACT>
