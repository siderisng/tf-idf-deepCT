<ABSTRACT>
Method for producing profiles with a stretch limit of ≧ 500 N / mm², a tensile strength of ≧ 600 N / mm² and high toughness of hot-rolled steel tape or sheet with the following process steps; a) the steel strip or sheet metal or sheet metal is made from a precipitation-curable fine-grain steel is heat-offened from the rolling heat after the last rolling stitch from an end rolling temperature above the A₁ temperature to a temperature of about 400 ° C and then cooled to room temperature; b) The steel tape or sheet is cold-processed to an open PRO fil; c) The profile is subjected to a solution annealing for the dissolution of the coarse precipitations and then cooled to the formation of finely divided precipitates (carbides, nitrides, carbonitrides). The method makes it possible to produce highly solid profiles in a simple and cost-viscant manner without expensive compensation, in particular pipe profiles, which are mechanically welding.
</ABSTRACT>
