<ABSTRACT>
a catalyst is described for use in a process for oxidizing  and/or cracking a heavy hydrocarbon under fluidizing condi­ tions in the presence of the catalyst, the process including  the steps of simultaneously or subsequently reducing iron  oxides in the catalyst and then reacting the reduced oxida­ tion-state iron with steam to produce hydrogen.  the catalyst  consists essentially of from 30 to 60 wt% fe, 0. 1 to 10 wt% ni,  and 10 wt% or less sio₂, and has a specific surface area of  from 0. 1 to 30 m²/g and an apparent specific gravity of from  2. 5 to 4. 0.
