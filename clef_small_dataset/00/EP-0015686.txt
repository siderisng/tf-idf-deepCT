<TITLE>
apparatus for supporting a travelling belt.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
apparatus (26, 72) in which lateral movement of a travel­ ling belt (10) is controlled so that the belt (10) moves in a pre­ determined path.  the apparatus includes at least one roller  (22) having a plurality of spaced-apart resilient fins (72) extend­ ing outwardly from the exterior surface thereof.  the portion of  the fins (72) contacting the belt (10) passing thereover pro­ vides support therefor.  preventing the belt (10) from deviating  laterally from the pre-determined path by more than a chosen  extent deflects the portion (72a) of the fins (72) in contact with  the belt (10).  the deflected portions (72a) of the fins (72) re­ turn to their normal positions after coming out of contact with  the belt.  in this manner, the maximum lateral force applied to  the edge of the belt (10) by deflection limiting means (26) never  exceeds the minimum force required to buckle the belt (10).  
</ABSTRACT>
