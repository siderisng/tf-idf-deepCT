<ABSTRACT>
1.  method for the electro-dynamic stirring of the molten core of a metal slab (1) running through a metallic support roller frame outside the casting chiller, wherein a magnetic field is generated by means of at least one winding and engages a portion of the slab (1) as well as associated support rollers (4) and an electric current is conductively led into and out of the slab (1) through support rollers (4), characterized thereby, that the magnetic flux (b) generated by the magnetic field is conducted through the metal slab (1) in such a manner and the electric current (i) flow through the metal slab (1) in such a manner that in the molten core (3) of the metal slab (1) at least two locally different and oppositely directed parallel driving forces (py) are generated, through which a guided circular stirring of the molten core (3) takes place in a plane parallel to the slab withdrawal direction (p). 
</ABSTRACT>
