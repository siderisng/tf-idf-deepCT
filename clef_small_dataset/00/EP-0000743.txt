<ABSTRACT>
The method for producing tantalum contacts on silicon semiconductor substrates is particularly suitable for the production of Schottky barrier film diodes with low potential threshold.The substrate is cleaned before applying the tantalum layer.Then, the tantalum layer (28) is deposited low pressure and low substrate temperature, thereby avoiding oxidation of the tantalum layer.Subsequently, the contact is sintered, whereby interfacial charges and between tantalum and substrate are eliminated existing films.When a metal is used during the processing, which reacts with silicon, such as aluminum for connecting lines, then a chromium layer (30) must be deposited between the tantalum layer (28) and the aluminum layer (32).
