<ABSTRACT>
the present invention relates to the field of the preservation of food products.  the device comprises a plurality of eggs which have been cooked in their shell, then peeled, an envelope closed in a leaktight manner inside which the said eggs are arranged and a carbon dioxide-rich gaseous medium possibly containing a substance which modifies or improves the organoleptic qualities of the egg, this envelope may be flexible, semirigid or rigid; the filling pressure is at most equal to approximately 7 bars.  the invention allows a preservation period of 3 to 5 weeks at +2 deg c.
