<ABSTRACT>
traditionally, the oxidation of black liquor from pulp  mills is carried out either before or after the black liquor is  concentrated in a multiple effect evaporation system.  appl­ icants have found that if the oxidation vessel (blox) is  positioned between adjacent effects (1) and (2) in multiple  effect evaporation systems or, alternatively, between the first  effect (1) and flash tank (flash) normally associated with  multiple effect evaporation system the heat released on oxi­ dation of the black liquor can be recovered as high value  energy (i. e.  steam) rather than low value energy (i. e.  warm  water) associated with traditional systems.
