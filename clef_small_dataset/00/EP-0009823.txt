<ABSTRACT>
in a tape measure, the cross-section of the measuring tape is curved.  in order to increase the resistance of the measuring tape to buckling or bending in the direction of bending in which the measuring tape has hitherto possessed a relatively low resistance to bending or buckling, the measuring tape (1) exhibits, close to or within the transverse curvature (3), at least one oppositely aligned counter- curvature (4).  advantageously, the measuring tape (1) is corrugated in cross-section, having two or three curvatures (3,4) of equal or different crown heights and breadths.  the transverse curvatures may exhibit flat cross-sectional areas (11,12).  <image>
