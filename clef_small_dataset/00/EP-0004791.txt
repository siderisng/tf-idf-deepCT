<ABSTRACT>
a supported titanium catalyst component for use in  propylene polymerization is prepared by copulverizing a  mixture of magnesium dichloride, titanium tetrachloride-ethylbenzoate  1:1 adduct and hexachloroethane for 20 to 44  hrs. , and then treating the resulting titanium-containing solid  with n-heptane solution of hexachloroethane.  bulk polymerization  of propylene in the presence of h₂ with the catalyst  composed of the supported titanium catalyst component and  the organo aluminum catalyst component composed of  triisobutyl aluminum and ethylbenzoate at 68°c.  for half an  hour gives higher catalyst efficiency (above 200,000 g  pp/g-ti) and sufficient heptane insolubles (92 to 97%).
