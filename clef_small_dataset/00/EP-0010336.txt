<ABSTRACT>
the novel bis(dithiocarbamate) salts having the formula    wherein n is the valence of ma and is 1,2 or 3, and m is  ammonium, an alkali metal, or a transition metal, especially  zinc or manganese, or having the formula    o-(ch₂ch₂nhs⊖)₂ xa yb   wherein x and y are not the same and both represent a  bivalent transistion metal cation, especially of zinc and man­ ganese, and a + v = 1, with the proviso that a and b are, indi­ vidually, greater than 0, or having the formula    o-(ch₂ch₂nhs⊖)₂  zn+2. (hnr′ r″)y   wherein r′ and r″ are, individually, either ch₂ch₂nh₂ or  ch₂ch₂oh and r′ and r″, taken together, is-(ch₂)₅; and y has  a value of from about 0,10 to 2, are useful as antifungal agents.   they are prepared in the usual manner by reacting bis-(2-­ aminoethyl) ether with carbon disulfide and  aqueous  ammonium or alkali metal hydroxide and recovering and  purifying the so produced water-solube bis- dithiocarba­ mates.  one of them is, if desired, reacted in aqueous solution  with a watersoluble salt of one or two transistion metal(s) for  producing the water-insoluble transition mono or mixed  metal salt(s).  the so produced zn-salt is, if desired, com­ plexed with variable amounts of an amine of the formula   hnr′r″,   especially (2-(2-aminoethyl) amino)ethanol or piperidine.
