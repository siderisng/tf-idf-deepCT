<ABSTRACT>
in agricultural machines, it is often necessary to drive the planting, processing and/or harvesting tools in synchronism with the driving speed of the machine or with a percentage lead or lag in relation to the driving speed of the machine.  in this agricultural machine, the object is, by the use of a non-driven wheel running on the ground, to provide a control device which within wide limits allows any desired speed or distance relationship, whilst maintaining the linear dependence on the driving speed, between the driving speed or driving distance and the rotational speed or distance of the working tool.  to achieve this object, there is a first inductive transmitter (16) connected operatively to the running wheel, for generating a distance-dependent or speed-dependent signal, and a second inductive transmitter (17) connected operatively to the working tool, for generating a signal corresponding to the distance or speed of the working tool.  electronics (18) serve for comparing the two signals and for entering additional correction signals.  only the signals are therefore treated and processed, and this can be carried out very easily by means of electronics.  the drive of the working tools is then varied accordingly by means of an adjusting device.  <image>
</ABSTRACT>
