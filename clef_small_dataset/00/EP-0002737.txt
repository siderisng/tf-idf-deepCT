<ABSTRACT>
sulfur is burned in oxygen at high temperatures and  high pressures without the use of a catalyst.  the product  gas, i. e. , so₃, so₂ and o₂, is condensed to separate part  of the so₂ and o₂ and then distilled to separate the remainder  and produce so₃ and/or oleum and sulfuric acid.   the so₂ and o₂ from the separator (3) and still (4) are  recycled to the burner (1).
