<ABSTRACT>
The bonding arrangement of an electric circuit breaker comprises a pawl and a locking tab with this cooperating lug, which is mounted by means of two bearing pins (50) in bearing openings (40) stationary parts. The bearing journals (50) have a rectangular cross-section and are arranged so that the force exerted by the pawl on the locking tab is transmitted to one of the narrow surfaces (51) of the bearing pin (50). The bearing openings (40) have a convex curved surface (43) cooperating with the narrow surface (51) of the bearing pin (50). The long surfaces (53,54) of the journal (50) are also compared to convex curved surfaces (48, 49) of the bearing openings (40). For the other narrow surface (52) of the bearing pins (50), a concave curved surface or a two angularly connected partial surfaces (45, 46) and through a transition sheet (47) is opposite surface (44). The bearing pins (50) cooperate with the bearing openings (40) by rolling and sliding movements associated with a low specific stress of the touch surfaces.
