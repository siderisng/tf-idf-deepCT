<ABSTRACT>
In a tabular insulating body consisting of adjacent, parallel mineral wool paint cells whose fibrous planes extend at right angles to the side surfaces of the body, the fins are held together by connecting means which are inserted in transverse to the longitudinal direction of the fins.The cuts have a low depth in terms of the strength of the slats.The connecting means may be made of creasers which are either, for producing a flexible insulating mat, in just one side surface or, for producing a dimensionally stable insulating board, in both side surfaces.A dimensionally stable panel can be further with the aid of connecting means in the form of strips, e.g.b.From corrugated cardboard, achieving with relatively low strength, which are used high-client into the cuts.
</ABSTRACT>
