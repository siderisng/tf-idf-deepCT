<ABSTRACT>
a unidirectional gear transmission of reduced axial thick­ ness advantageously employed in timepieces is adapted to be  driven by a stepping motor.  the driving gear (1) is provided  with an alternating pattern of teeth (12, 13) having a first  thickness and spacing and sets of two teeth (3, 4) having  greater thickness and spacing.  the driven gear (2) is provided  with an alternating pattern of teeth (10, 11) corresponding to  the first thickness teeth (12, 13) of the driving gear (1) and two  spaces (6, 7) corresponding to the greater thickness teeth (3, 4)  of the driving gear (1) separated by a single tooth (9).  regular  stepping rotation in either sense can be transmitted from the  driving gear (1) to the driven gear (2), but transmission of  rotation from driven (2) to driving (1) gear is prevented.  
</ABSTRACT>
