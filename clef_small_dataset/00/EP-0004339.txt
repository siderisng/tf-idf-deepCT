<ABSTRACT>
the process functions in the presence of nitrogen and/or nitrogen-containing compounds in an electrolysator at a voltage, applied to the electrodes of the electrolyser, of from 2 to 1000 volt and at a current density of from 0. 001 to 10 ampere/mm<2>.  it makes possible (a) the preparation of dinitrogen dioxide in comparatively high concentrations mixed with hydrogen and nitrogen at the cathode of an electrolyser, with or without oxygen, and the useful application of gas mixtures containing or consisting of dinitrogen oxide and hydrogen.  it further makes possible (b) the winning of large energy quantities by thermo-electrical dissociation of dinitrogen oxide and also the utilisation of these energy quantities.    figure 6 shows an electrolyser comprising a container (1), an anode (3) with a ring-shaped part (6), a cathode (4) with a ring-shaped part (7), a rectifier (5) and the electrolyte (2).  <image>
