<TITLE>
preparation of salt-free n-acyl taurines.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>
gaf corpus<sep>gaf corporation<sep>gaf corporation140 west 51st streetnew york new york 10020us<sep>
</APPLICANT>
<INVENTOR>
chakrabarti paritosh mohanus<sep>chakrabarti, paritosh mohan<sep>chakrabarti, paritosh mohan1149 ratzer roadwayne new jersey 07470us<sep>
</INVENTOR>
<ABSTRACT>
a process for preparing a substantially saltfree n-acyl  taurine by reacting 1 mode of a higher molecular weight  carboxylic acid chloride with 1 mole of a taurine salt containing  at least 1 n-bonded h atom in a lower alkanol  initially containing no more than about 15% by weight  of water and about 1 mole of an alkali metal hydroxide at  elevated temperatures, preferably at reflux, separating the  insoluble precipitated alkali metal chloride from the  alkanolic reaction medium at a temperature high enough  to solubilize the n-acyl taurine reaction product, and  cooling the reaction medium low enough to precipitate the  desired n-acyl taurine.  
</ABSTRACT>
