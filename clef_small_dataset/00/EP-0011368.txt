<ABSTRACT>
in a nicotine transfer process a donor tobacco is conÂ­ tacted with a receiving substrate which has been treated with  a strong acid or an ammonium salt of a strong acid.  part of the  nicotine in the donor tobacco is transferred from the donor  tobacco to the receiving substrate.  thereafter the donor  tobacco and the substrate may be separated.  as a result the  donor tobacco has a reduced nicotine content and hence is  more desirable for use in a smoking product.  where the  receiving substrate is a low nicotine tobacco by-product (or  artificial smoking material) said substrate, enriched in  nicotine as a result of the transfer, may be used as a filler  material for smoking products.
