<ABSTRACT>
a recording material comprises a transparent support  (e. g.  a polyethylene terephthate, polycarbonate, p. v. c. , polys­ tyrene or triacetate transparent film) having formed thereon a  recording layer containing a coagulatable proteinaceous  compound, such as albumin, haemoglobin, globulin, prola­ min or glutelin.  the layer may also contain a dye, pigment, an  antiseptic or mold-proofing agent and/or a film forming  agent.  the layer has an average optical density of at least  about 1. 5 in the wavelength region of about 350 nm to 450 nm.   the recording material is used to prepare a reprographic  material such as a mask by a short flash exposure thereof in  heat conducting relationship with an original and then by  removal of portions of the recording layer not rendered insol­ uble.  
</ABSTRACT>
