<ABSTRACT>
a rotary latch and its method of operation, the latch being  caused to move between a position flush with a surrounding  surface and a protruding position as the latch is moved bet­ ween its latched position and its unlatched position by a  rotary drive means (14); the drive means (14) by reason of its  protruding position, being readily visible to indicate the  unlatched condition; the rotary drive means (14) including a  control sleeve having radially outwardly facing detent pockets  (15, 16, 17) engaged by a radially movable pin (23), one socket  (15) being engaged when the latch is in its flush-latched condi­ tion, a second socket (17) being engaged when in its fully  unlatched condition, and a third socket (16) being engaged as  a secondary latch.
