<ABSTRACT>
The spectrum of the voice signal initially constituted by 12-bit encoded SN samples, appearing at a frequency of 8 kHz is split into two bands.one serves to generate (calc) of the partial self-correlation coefficients providing after quantization (in QA2) information coef, the other, the lowest, is on the one hand, split (in LPFD andSBC) in quantified subbands with a dynamic setting of the bit rate assigned to each subband so as to provide such signal information, and on the other hand used (EN and QA1) for a tape energy measurementhigh providing an ENERG information.The ENERG, SIGNAL and COEF data characterize the recoded voice signal.They can be multiplexed (by MPX) on the same path of transmission.
