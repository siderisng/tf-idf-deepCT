<ABSTRACT>
a board product containing glassy inorganic fibre  such as mineral wool, in a matrix of a plastic clay such  as ball clay.  the amounts of clay and inorganic fibre are  in the respective ranges 29 to 80 per cent by weight and  15 to 55 per cent by weight of the product, and its den­ sity is at least 500 kg/m³.  the board can replace asbestos millboard and hat the  advantage of being asbestos-free.  it can be made by the  standard paper and board-making techniques, when pre­ ferred ingredients are pulp fibres e. g.  cellulose pulp, and  a complementary binder, particularly hydrolysable starch,  the latter enabling the product to be re-mouldable upon  moistening with water.  
</ABSTRACT>
