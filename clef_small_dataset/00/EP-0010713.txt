<TITLE>
process for the purification of waste water containing organic compounds of sulfur and phosphor.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>
bayer agde <sep>bayer ag <sep>bayer ag 51368 leverkusende<sep>
</APPLICANT>
<INVENTOR>

<ABSTRACT>
the waste water is oxidised at a redox potential between 300 and 600 mv (measured with a platinum electrode relative to a silver-silver chloride electrode) at temperatures of from between 20 and 220 deg c and at oxygen partial pressures of 1 to 10 bar in the presence of such amounts of iron(ii) salt and optionally calcium ions that virtually the entire phosphorus present in the waste water is converted into solid iron(iii) phosphate. 
</ABSTRACT>
