<TITLE>
correction circuit for byte-structured errors.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
in a correction device for correcting byte-structured errors, a gmc code is used.  for the purpose of decoding, the error syndromes are split area by area into two part syndromes which can be considered as elements  alpha <i> of galois fields gf(2<r>).  the bits of the part-syndromes are used for addressing two read-only memories each which contain the exponents i of the coefficients  alpha <i>.  the modulo 2<r>-1 difference from the contents read out of the read-only memories due to a particular error syndrome directly specify the location of the faulty byte in purely binary coding.  <image>
