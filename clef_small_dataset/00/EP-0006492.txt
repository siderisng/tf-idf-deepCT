<TITLE>
method of digital interpolation of one period of a three-phased analog signal.

<ABSTRACT>
The values ​​of the three components of the analog signal are compared with zero and two among each other. Each possible combination of the comparison results is assigned a zone in the area of ​​the phase arguments of the analog signal each, resulting in a coarse-inter polation of a period of the analog signal in zones. The components are digitized and the components with the smallest (x), the middle (y) and the largest (z) absolute value are selected. The digital functions H = Y - X and K = Z - Y + X are formed. Each possible combination (H, K) is each assigned a certain number of steps of the phase argument within half of the zone, resulting in a fine-inter polation of the zone in steps. Preferably, a third of the sum of the three components is subtracted from each analog component. The method is applied in particular to the interpolation of three-phase measurement signals from photocells in the angle measuring in trajectory measurement theodolites.
</ABSTRACT>
