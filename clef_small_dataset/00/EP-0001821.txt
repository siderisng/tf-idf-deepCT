<ABSTRACT>
In a method for the racemate cleavage of DL-α-aminocarboxylic acids, these are converted into a salt in an inert solvents with an aromatic O-hydroxy aldehyde to an azomethine derivative and this at the same stage with an optically active amine base.The diastereomeric salts formed are separated and obtained from acid treatment therefrom the respective optically active α-aminocarboxylic acid.Aliphatic and aromatic α-aminocarboxylic acids can be columns, in particular p-hydroxy-c-phenylglycine.
