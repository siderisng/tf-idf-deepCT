<ABSTRACT>
when recovering resorcinol from high-boiling by­ products formed by the acid-cleavage of  m-diisopropylbenzene dihydroperoxide by thermal cracking  in the presence of ammonium sulfate and/or ammonium  bisulfate at a temperature of 170°c to 350°c under reduced  pressure, increased amounts of resorcinol can be recovered if  thermal cracking is carried out at a pressure of less than 50  mmhg gauge in the absence of any supplied inert gas.
