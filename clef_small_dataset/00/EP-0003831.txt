<ABSTRACT>
1.  a process for burning salt-laden residues in a fluidised bed at temperatures in the range of from 730 to 900 degrees c with removal of fluidised bed mass, characterized in that, before a salt concentration at which the fluidised-bed mass cakes at the working temperature is reached in the fluidised-bed mass, part of the fluidised-bed mass is removed and replaced by fresh fluidisation stock.
