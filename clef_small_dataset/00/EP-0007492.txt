<ABSTRACT>
the receptacle (112) is formed of a floor (116) and four walls which extend from the latter and of which two opposite walls are transparent, flat and parallel to one another and have a predetermined spacing from one another.  the other two side walls (119) possess a smaller spacing from one another, so that between the four side walls a space is formed with a narrow, rectangular cross-section to receive a small quantity of liquid.  the two more closely compacted side walls (119) carry at their external surfaces two respective ribs, which extend parallel to the other two transparent side walls.  the outer edges of these ribs are connected to one another by walls to form a respective receiving pocket (117) for the limbs of a u-shaped heat-conducting body to be inserted from below.  each one of these two walls is penetrated by a narrow slit (120), which extends in a vertical direction from the lower end of the receptacle to the vicinity of the upper end of the pocket (117) and is narrow in comparison with the width of the wall.  <image>
