<ABSTRACT>
a process for deriving certain o,o-dialkyl phosphorochloridothioates  in the substantial absence of contamination  by free sulfur and avoiding difficult-tohandle sulfur  distillation residue, in which process distillation to recover  substantially pure product is carried out in the present of a  sulfur-solubilizing agent.  the process may be used in  separating the desired product from a mixture thereof with  sulfur produced by reaction of phosphorus pentasulfide  and an alkanol, chlorinating and converting by-product  sulfur monochloride to free sulfur.  the agent is a liquid  aromatic compound having at least ten carbon atoms and  selected from alkylated and/or chlorinated naphthalenes  and anthracenes, and naphthalene, diphenyl and terphenyls,  preferably a naphthalene or naphthalenic oil.  the product o,o-dialkyl phosphorochloridothioates  have utility in, for example, the preparation of insecticides.  
</ABSTRACT>
