<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a two-stage method for electrolytic metal salt coloring of aluminum, wherein a oxide layer generated by DC in acidic solution is colored by alternating current by a tin (II) salts of acidic acid electrolytes.The electrolyte according to the invention contains 1 - 10 g / l iron (II) salts of the pigic acid, a sulfonic acid with a maximum of 8 C atoms or the amidosulfonic acid.The electrolyte preferably contains iron (II) sulfosalicylate and / or iron (II) sulfone phthalate and optionally additionally 1 - 10 g / l sulfosalicylic acid and / or sulfophthalic acid.The additions prevent the formation of precipitates in the electrolyte while standing.In addition, in many cases, a significant color-intensifier-kender effect is achieved.
