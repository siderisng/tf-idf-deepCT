<ABSTRACT>
The object of the invention is to stabilize in temperature, by simple means, one or more points of an electrical circuit, and again to provide a thermostatic connector to one or more connecting jacks.For this purpose, we put the point or the jack in good thermal contact with a resistor with a positive temperature coefficient, optionally surrounded by a second analog resistance, these resistors being powered energized.For example, a connector (in section FIG. 12) constituted by a metal casing (101), surrounding a sleeve-shaped resistor (105), itself surrounding a thermally conductive material in which is drowned at least one Jack (106).Application to electrical measurements especially by thermo-couples.
