<ABSTRACT>
Electric optical display device for displaying dark symbols on a light surface, in which both the front and back plate almost completely with only by narrow recesses (A, a ', b, b') separate partial electrodes (A, b, c 'D, E, F, G, H, I, J) is covered whose surpluses form the optionally controllable display elements. This device is particularly suitable for cholesterol liquid crystal substances provided with a pleochroic dye as well as electrophoretic suspensions as electro-optical media and requires no polarizers. The electrical control of this device is carried out by such addressing of the electrodes (A, B, C, J) that at the display elements selected by this addressing (A ', B', C ') is a vanishing potential difference and therefore these display elements remain dark While at all other partial areas of the electrode surface, a potential difference (2V, V) is greater than the transition voltage (UC) of the electro-optical medium, so that they appear as a light surface. Control is preferably carried out by unipolar pulse signals based on the partial electrodes of the front and back plate, which determine the selected display elements, with the same phase and amplitude.
