<TITLE>
producing printed circuits by conjoining metal powder images.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
by the process of this invention, printed circuits are  prepared containing an electrically conductive wiring trace  from materials having adherent and non-adherent surface  areas, e. g. , printed circuit substrates bearing an imaged  photoadhesive layer.  onto the adherent surface areas of the  material are applied ductile metal or alloy particles, and any  excess particles are removed from the non-adherent areas.   the metallized areas are conjoined, e. g. , with silicon carbide  brush or rounded metal rod.  the conjoined areas can be  electrolessly plated, electroplated or soldered.  multilayer  printed circuits can also be prepared by repeating these  steps with additional layers of photoadhesive material  adhered to underlying printed circuits.  
</ABSTRACT>
