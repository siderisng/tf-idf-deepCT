<ABSTRACT>
The reverse grouping of a multi-stage coupling field of a PCM switching system consists of a first (time) coupling stage (A) formed from individual coupling groups (G1 to Gm) which are summarized to a large group (GG), a second (room) coupling stage ( B1) consisting of as many PCM lines such as coupling groups (G1 to Gm) within a large group (GG), wherein each a coupling group is connected outgoing to a PCM line, while a coupling group-specific multiplexer (M) has access of all PCM lines to each of a coupling group (G) and the third (room) coupling stage (B2) is formed with each one of a multi-higher bit rate of the PCM lines of the second (room) coupling stage which is designed for just as many PCM lines how large groups (GG) are present. In the third coupling stage (B2), there is an access to all PCM pipes of the second coupling stage (B1) in each large group (GG), while there is access to the individual coupling groups of the first coupling stage (A). To adapt the faster third coupling stage (B2) to the slower coupling stages (A) and (B1), each large group (GG) is equipped with the necessary bit rate converters (BRV, BRR).
</ABSTRACT>
