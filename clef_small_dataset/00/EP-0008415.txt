<ABSTRACT>
1.  a dental patient's chair, having a pivotably mounted back rest which essentially consists of a -shaped support part (6) the crosspiece (10) of which adjoins the rear edge of the chair seat (3) and is connected to the chair seat by means of a hinge (5), and of a cushion layer (9) arranged on the support part, and which back rest has in its lower half, a free space which is directed towards the central axis of the back rest and has a width (a) in the direction of the longitudinal axis of the chair, of at least the width of the knee of an average person, and said chair having an arm rest (16) which is connected by means of a knuckle joint, on the one hand, to the back rest (4), and, on the other hand, to the seat (3), characterized in that the central bar (11) of the support part (6) extends to the upper edge of the back and on the central bar rests the face of a relatively thin cushion layer (9) which corresponds to the width (b) of the back and extends to the surface of the seat, and that, in the upper half of the back, lateral support parts (7, 8) of the same size are secured to the central bar (11) on either side thereof, at least one of said lateral support parts containing a bush (19) for the arm rest (16) and the other one thereof being detachably secured to the central bar (11).
