<TITLE>
metallised aromatic polyamide fibres; process for metallising polyamides.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>
bayer ag de  <sep>bayer ag<sep>bayer ag51368 leverkusende<sep>
</APPLICANT>
<INVENTOR>
ebneth harold drde<sep>ebneth, harold, dr.<sep>ebneth, harold, dr.berta-von-suttner-strasse 61d-5090 leverkusen 1de<sep>
</INVENTOR>
<ABSTRACT>
fibres, yarns or textile sheet materials made of aramids are metallised by introducing them into a colloidal palladium solution containing acidic tin(ii) ions in excess, treating the thus activated material with an acid or alkali and then coating it electrolessly with metal at room temperature in a basic metal salt solution.  the metallised fibres, yarns or textile sheet materials of aromatic polyamides have a surface resistance at 50% relative humidity and 23 deg c, measured in accordance with din 54 345, of not more than 1 x 10<3> omega  when the metal coat has a thickness of 0. 11  mu m and of not more than 3 x 10<1> omega  when the metal coat has a thickness of 0. 19  mu m. 
</ABSTRACT>
