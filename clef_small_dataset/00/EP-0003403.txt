<ABSTRACT>
the invention relates to thermally insulating, fire resistant  material and its production.  a problem associated with fire resistant material comprising  refractory material and a binder is the tendency, in  use, of the material to disintegrate due to bond failure.  in the  invention, hollow particles of a glass or of a ceramic are  combined with a binder to give a material where the  proportions of binder are such that the material is capable of  deformation at high temperatures (e. g.  up to 1100Â°c) without  fracture or loss of cohesion.  a specific example consists of  93% by weight of hollow spherical particles of pulverised fuel  ash and 7% by weight of sodium silicate binder.  the material  is particularly useful in the protection of steel structures.
