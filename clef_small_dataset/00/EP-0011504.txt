<ABSTRACT>
the electrodeposition of a protein directly onto an elec­ trode suffers from a variety of problems such as reactions  between the protein and the electrodes and gases produced  by electrolysis of water at the electrode surfaces.  in order to  overcome these problems, at least one ion-exchange mem­ brane (4,4′) is placed between the electrodes (2,3) and the  protein is caused to be deposited on one of the membranes  by electrophoresis.  this technique can be used to produce  films of protein.
