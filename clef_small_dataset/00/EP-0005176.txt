<ABSTRACT>
In the process for producing 1,2-dichloroethane by oxychlorination of ethylene with hydrogen chloride and a molecular oxygen-containing gas in the gas phase at elevated temperature in the presence of a catalyst consisting of copper II chloride on a carrier in the fluidized bed, wherein the reaction gases are Cooling pressure into two condensation stages, decades 1,2-dichloroethane and water, while unreacted output gases and inert gases are predominantly guided in circulation, such that the reaction gases of the 2nd condensation stage in a 3rd condensation stage under pressure only as far as between 5 and 18 ° C cooled temperature that still 0.5 to 3 volume% of 1,2-dichloroethane remain in the circulatory gas, and that the consumed oxygen is supplemented by supply pure oxygen directly to the circulatory gas in front of the reactor, one directs the preheated hydrogen chloride stream completely or partially in the up to 150-200 ° C. On the circulatory gas stream and then leads the pure oxygen to the circulatory gas stream between the feedstay of the hydrogen chloride and the entrance point of the circulatory gas into the reactor.
