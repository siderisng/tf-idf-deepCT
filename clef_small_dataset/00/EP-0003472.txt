<ABSTRACT>
In a method of cleavage of hydrochloride from amine hydrochlorides, an amine hydrochloride whose amine component is heated from tertiary alkylamines, tertiary aryl dial cylamines, secondary arylalkylamines, primary alkylarylamines or mixtures thereof which contain 14 to 36 carbon atoms in the nitrogen-bonded side chains, at most a nitrogen-bonded methyl group and at least a nitrogen-bonded aliphatic radical containing at least 6 carbon atoms consists in an inert organic, largely non-polar solvent, under passing an inert gas stream to 100 to 250 ° C. As inert solvents, above 120 ° C, one uses boiling straight-chain or branched aliphatic hydrocloaliphatic, aromatic or araliphatic hydrocarbons as well as mixtures of these compounds or corresponding petroleum fractions, further open-chain dialkyl ethers and cyclic ethers, cyclic ketones and in α-position branched ketones, saturated or unsaturated perchlorinated Aliphatic hydrocarbons as well as chlorine, dichloro or nitrobenzene.
