<ABSTRACT>
Methods for condensation of solids, in particular phosphates which tend to sinteres and / or glue during condensation, in which the solids subjected to the condensation mixes in a reactor in a thin layer to a heated reactor wall and again the condensed hard layer again the reactor wall peels and brings back to the reactor interior. The reactor wall is preferably heated by means of a salt or molten metal, the heat transfer into the interior of the reactor takes place by peeling the hot condensed layer. The apparatus for carrying out this method consists of a heated heat-containing reactor housing containing a metal or salt melt with inner star-shaped mixing tool with paddling, wherein at least one paddle is replaced by a switching knife, which is arranged in a small distance from the reactor wall. The reactor housing is preferably trough-shaped and freely movably suspended in the longitudinal direction. For carrying out condensation reactions in which the temperature is to be variable via the course of the reaction, a device is used which has a plurality of separate heating pockets that are fed with heating media of different temperature.
</ABSTRACT>
