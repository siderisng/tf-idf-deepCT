<ABSTRACT>
an electronically tunable am/fm radio receiver has a tun­ ing means (33-37) for scanning both fm and am local oscilla­ tion frequencies.  a common circuit (33) used in both fm re­ ception and am reception is provided to stop automatically the  scanning of the associated local oscillation frequency at its op­ timum point when a radio signal is received.  the circuit (33) in­ cludes a converter (334, 335) for converting fm intermediate  frequency signals into signals of the am intermediate frequen­ cy, and a detector (338) which can operate in response both to  the converted fm signals and to am intermediate frequency  signals from an am front stage (20) for detecting the received  radio signal.  there may be a local oscillator (203) for am recep­ tion and a separate local oscillator (103) for fm reception, or  the local oscillation signals may be provided by a common os­ cillator.  
</ABSTRACT>
