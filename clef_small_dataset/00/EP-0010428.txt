<ABSTRACT>
a process for producing a random terpolymer of ethylene  and alpha-olefins of at least 3 carbon atoms in the presence of  a catalyst composed of (1) a titanium catalyst component  containing at least magnesium and titanium and (2) an  organoaluminum catalyst component in a hydrocarbon sol­ vent at a temperature above the melting point of the ter­ polymer formed, under such conditions that the resulting  terpolymer dissolves in the hydrocarbon solvent; character­ ized in that said terpolymer consists essentially of   (a) more than 90 mole% to 99. 5 mole% of ethylene.    (b) 0. 2 mole% to 9. 8 mole% of an alpha-olefin of 3 or 4  carbon atoms, and   (c) 0. 2 mole% to 9. 8 mole% of an alpha-olefin of 5 to 18  carbon atoms,   the total of the proportions of the monomers (a), (b) and (c)  being 100 mole%.  the resulting terpolymer has good  mechanical properties and is also transparent, and thus finds  particular utility in the production of melt-shaped articles,  particularly packaging films.  
</ABSTRACT>
