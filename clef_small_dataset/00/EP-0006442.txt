<ABSTRACT>
In a balancable thin film resistor, the thin film (10) applied to a substrate is arranged in a small ge geometry determined by a low ratio of length.The length corresponds to the current path between the electrodes (12, 13) of the resistor.The adjustment is carried out by laser sector (15) through which the thin film (10) is divided into partial squares.The partial surface quadrates contributing to the current flow, which connect the electrodes (12, 13) in a serial or parallel arrangement, determine the respective resistance value.In this way, in the case of the smallest possible surface, the highest possible variation width can be achieved with regard to the substitute values.
