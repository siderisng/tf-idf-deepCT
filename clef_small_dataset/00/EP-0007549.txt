<ABSTRACT>
a phase-angle adjusting device for electricity meters having a short-circuit winding (3) surrounding both legs of the current core (1) is constructed in such a manner that the short-circuit winding (3) surrounding the two legs and the adjustment loop (6) located in series with the former is punched out of a single-part plate having a positive temperature coefficient and is shaped in such a manner that the fluxes in the two legs of the current core (1) are uniformly loaded when the resistance of the adjustment loop (6) is changed by means of a slidable contact bridge (9).  due to the symmetric loading of the fluxes in the two legs of the current core (1), the temperature-dependent voltage and current no-load drives are prevented.  this is why copper can also be used as a material, the positive temperature coefficient of which increases or decreases the resistance value of the adjusting device with rising or, respectively, falling temperature.  this results in a temperature-dependent change in the angle of adjustment so that the temperature-dependent phase-angle error of the meter is reduced.  <image>
</ABSTRACT>
