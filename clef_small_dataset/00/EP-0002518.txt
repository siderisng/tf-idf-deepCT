<ABSTRACT>
1.  condensation product of epsilon-caprolactam, formaldehyde and formamide in a molar ratio of 1 : a : b, where a is a number from 1 to 20, b is a number from 1 to 19 with the provise that the quotient of a/b+1 is 0. 5 to 1, obtained by heating formamide and formaldehyde in the presence of a base and optionally of epsilon-caprolactam at temperatures of 70 to 90 degrees c for a 4 to 15 hours, acidifying the reaction batch, adding epsilon-caprolactam, if not previously present, and distilling off water at temperatures of 100 to 135 degrees c.
