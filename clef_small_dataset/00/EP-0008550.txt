<ABSTRACT>
1.  a coated wire for welding and building up with a deposit of a cobalt-base alloy containing at most 36 % of chromium, consisting firstly of a sheath of ductile alloy having a cobalt content higher than 90 % and secondly of a metallic powder filling the tubular sheath, characterized by the fact that the metallic powder is entirely formed of grains of homogeneous composition containing more than 12 % of cobalt and less than 4. 5 % of carbon, less than 3. 5 % of silicon, 55 to 81 % of chromium, 0 to 20 % of tungsten, 0 to 14 % of molybdenum and 0 to 8 % of nickel and represents 41 to 50 % of the total weight of the coated wire, the elements cr, w, mo and ni not entering into the composition of the sheath. 
</ABSTRACT>
