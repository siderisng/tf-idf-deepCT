<ABSTRACT>
1.  box for packing and transporting objects, of the type comprising a bag comprising a rectangular or square bottom (12, 13, 14a, 14b, 15a, 15b), two main opposite faces (1 and 2) and two lateral gussets (3 and 8) and provided with gripping means (17, 20, 21, 22) at its part opposite said bottom, this box being made by folding and gluing a single sheet of a rigid material such as cardboard and said gripping means being constituted by a system of openings (20, 21, 22) and flap (17), forming an integral part of said sheet and bringing together the top edges of the opening of the bag and ensuring closure thereof, said gussets (3 and 8) being provided with longitudinal lines of fold (4c and 9c), characterised in that each gusset is provided with an intermediate assembly of lines of fold (4d to 4g and 9d to 9g) located between the bottom and the top part of the box, symmetrical in two's with respect to the corresponding longitudinal line of fold (4c or 9c), the intermediate assemblies of lines of fold being similar and disposed at the same height from the bottom of the box so that said box may be given, as desired, either a section continuously tapering from the bottom (12, 13, 14a, 14b, 15a, 15b) towards the gripping means (17, 20, 21, 22), or a constant section between the bottom (12, 13, 14a, 14b, 15a, 15b) and said intermediate assemblies of lines of fold (4d to 4g and 9d to 9g), then a section tapering continuously from said intermediate assemblies of lines of fold (4d to 4g and 9d to 9g) towards the gripping means (17, 20, 21, 22).
