<ABSTRACT>
Process for the preparation of amorphous crystallization capable sodium aluminum silicates by continuously mixing a sodium aluminate with a sodium silicate solution in a stepped mixed distance, wherein the two solutions to be mixed a computational total composition with respect to the molar ratios of 1.5 to 9 Na₂O: 1 Al₂O₃: 1 to 7 SiO₂: 40 to 400 H₂O have. In this case, one of the two reaction compenses are divided into a plurality of partial streams, one of which is continuously added to the total stream of the other reaction components immediately in cash, the further in each case current downwards the current of the formed reaction mixture is also continuously dosed continuously. As a stepped mixing distance, stirred beads can be found cascades, stepped executed differential screw mixers or stirring columns used, which are divided into at least as much stages, as the split reaction has component to partial currents. In a corresponding overall composition of the two solutions to be mixed to mix, the amorphous sodium aluminum silicate formed can then crystallize to a zeolite molecular sieve of type Naa.
