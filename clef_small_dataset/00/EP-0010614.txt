This method makes it possible to use fittings in the printing technical range of the core reactor, where high level of security levels can be used, such as sliders, from up to about 1000 mm nominal diameters, by making the slide housing (1) made of forging steel. The warm block is first seamlessly deformed in a template to a union open, tubular output body. At the open end then a material thickening (6) is immersed in order to produce a cylindrical part (39) with a larger diameter. The underlying part is flattened so that two parallel walls (23) are released. These are each provided with one each other coaxial by refraction, which are closed by collar (10, 11) of Ge Häuberohrstutzen (8, 9). At the collar circumference, the welding (12, 13) of the nozzle (8, 9) with the housing (1). The internal bearing ends (14, 15) which projecting inner projecting in the housing form the torment surfaces (16, 17) for the shut-off (3). Thanks to the housing flattening and the resulting good cause of the welds (12, 13), these can also be tested during operation by ultrasonic and X-ray devices.
