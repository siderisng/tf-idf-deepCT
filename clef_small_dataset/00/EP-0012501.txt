<ABSTRACT>
acid whey, the by-product from the manufacture of  fresh cheeses such as cottage cheese, is clarified, filtered  and subjected to lactose hydrolysis, splitting the iactose  disaccharide into the monosaccharides glucose and galac­ tose.  the liquid is sterilized and cultured with saccharo­ myces yeast e. g.  baker's yeast and used as a growth me­ dium for that yeast.  after yeast growth is substantially com­ pleted the yeast solids are separated and the liquid re­ maining is discharged into waste-water receiving systems,  the liquid significantly reduced in organic waste loading as  compared to untreated acid wheys.  
</ABSTRACT>
