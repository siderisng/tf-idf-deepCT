<ABSTRACT>
The thermostatization of a liquid stream comprises a liquid main exchanger (4) for the liquid, a heat-auxiliary exchanger (7) by which an auxiliary liquid is passed for preheating or pre-cooling, and a Peltier element (6) disposed between both exchangers (6), This serves with the aid of an electrical control device (12) for precisely controlling the temperature of the liquid amount flowing in the main exchanger to the desired value. For controlling the controller, a sensor (8) for the temperature of the liquid to be thermostatized is mounted in the main exchanger (4). Main exchanger (4), Peltier element (6) and auxiliary exchangers (7) have flat, disk-shaped shape and are arranged side by side in a tight thermal contact in this order side by side. In hemorrhageometry, this thermostat serves to constant a certain flowing amount of distilled water passed through a standard wound. Depending on the bleeding behavior, the rinsing liquid takes more or less blood with. In a connected photometer (3), the blood content is measured to determine the diagnostically significant bleeding behavior.
