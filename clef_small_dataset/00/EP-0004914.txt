<ABSTRACT>
process for producing compounds useful in perfumery  including isochromans and acylated indane hydrocarbons by  reacting the corresponding substituted benzene with an  alkylene oxide or with a lower acyl halide in the presence of a  hydrocarbon or hydrocarbon mixture containing c₅ to c₁₀  alkanes at a temperature of at least -20°c; preferably up to  -5°c.  in the case of forming isochromans, the resulting  alcohol is reacted, in situ, with a lower alkanol and a  formaldehyde precursor at temperatures of from 20°c up to  80°c.
