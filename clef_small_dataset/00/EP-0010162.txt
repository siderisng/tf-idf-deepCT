Methods for producing alkanesulfonic acid chlori The 1-12 C atoms in which the reaction of the corresponding alkanthiol and / or dialkyl disulfides with chlorine and water is carried out at a temperature between -10 and + 50 ° C in the desired alkanesulfonic acid chloride as a reaction medium.The reaction may be carried out batchwise or consequent in a circulation reactor, the circulation being effected by the hydrogen chloride formed in the reaction.
