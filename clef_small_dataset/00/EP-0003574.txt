<TITLE>
method for positioning and supplying a stock of cups or closures, or longish tubular or bottle-like containers or the like to a filling machine.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
the tray, which is designed as a rectangular prism, is provided on two opposite side parts with stabilising tabs (6, 7), the free edges of which are turned towards one another.  the stabilising tabs (6, 7) run essentially parallel to the bottom (1) of the tray.  with the aid of the tray, a machine can be supplied with cups or the like which are stacked into columns.  the trays are to this end placed on a feed channel to be emptied; the side part running parallel to the longitudinal direction of the columns is torn away so that the columns run automatically out of the tray and can be supplied to a filling machine via a chain conveyor and a conveyor belt (fig.  3).  <image>
