<ABSTRACT>
an exhaust gas sensor comprises a first exhaust gas  sensing element (46), composed, for example, of titania,  responsive to temperature and to the partial pressure of  oxygen, and a second similar metal oxide ceramic element  (48) which also is responsive to exhaust gas sensor tempera­ ture, but is more slowly responsive to the partial pressure of  oxygen in the exhaust gases.  changes in partial pressure of  oxygen are detected by connecting the sensor elements  (46,48) series with each other and with a source of electrical  energy (60) and detecting the voltage change across one of  the elements.  resistances (62,rb) connected in series with the  second element and/or in parallel therewith act to inhibit the  reduction in the magnitude of the change in voltage signal  produced in the sensor circuitry which would otherwise result  from prolonged exposure of the sensor ceramic elements to  exhaust gases produced by combustion of a rich air/fuel  mixture or a lean air/fuel mixture.
