<ABSTRACT>
To prepare the exposure of an X-ray-sensitive photoresist layer in the manufacture of integrated semiconductor circuits, an X-ray-sensitive layer (2), a heavy metal layer (3) and an electron-beam-sensitive layer (4) and an electron-beam-sensitive layer (4) is applied on a semiconductor sheet (1). Then, the electron beam-sensitive layer (4) is exposed by electron beams with the desired pattern. By developing the relevant regions of the heavy metal layer (3) are exposed and then removed by an etching process. The resulting heavy metal mask is firmly connected to the semiconductor tile (1) by the X-ray-sensitive layer (2). Since the thickness of the layer (2) amount to about 1 to 3 μm can be maintained by applying conventional best sealing methods with an accuracy of ± 5% and better constant, at its exposure, X-rays can also be used in a relatively large area incident angles, without that a distortion or transfer of the transmitted pattern occurs.
