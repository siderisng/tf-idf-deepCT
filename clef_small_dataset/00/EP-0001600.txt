<ABSTRACT>
a process for removing bis-chloromethyl ether and  1,1,1,2-tetrachloroethane from a mixture comprising these  two materials and chloroacetyl chloride by contacting the  mixture with a catalytic amount of ferric and/or aluminium  cations at a temperature from 50°c to 140°c and a residence  time of from 15 to 120 minutes.  this process can remove  bis-chloromethyl ether to a concentration of less than 0. 5  ppm and 1,1,1,2-tetrachloroethane to a concentration of 0. 05  weight percent, both based on the chloroacetyl chloride.
