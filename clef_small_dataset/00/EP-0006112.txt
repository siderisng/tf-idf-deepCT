<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for the preparation of thermosetting urea-formaldehyde resins with a molar ratio of urea to formaldehyde from 1: 1 to 1: 1.5, wherein in a three-stage process urea and formaldehyde first in the alkaline, then in the acidic and again in the alkaline A range is condensed. In this case, the urea / formaldehyde ratio in the first stage is 1: 1.5 to 1: 4 and is set to the ratio 1: 1 to 1: 1.5 in the third stage. The urea-formaldehyde resin is condensed up to a precipitation number of 1: 0.5 to 1: 3. The resulting resins have an improved penetration behavior over support paths. The resins are improved with respect to their hydrophilicity. Another object of the invention is therefore the use of the processed urea-formaldehyde resins for impregnating and / or coating support paths intended for the surface compensation of wood-based material plates.
