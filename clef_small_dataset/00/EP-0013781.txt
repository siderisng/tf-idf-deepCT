<ABSTRACT>
Chloro-oxygen acids or their salts are prepared by electrolysis of salt solutions or sea water contaminated with calcium and / or magnesium.In the course of the operating time, approaches or encrustations on the electrodes occur, especially in the first cells adjacent to the seawater.To avoid such encrustations, the electrolysis a) in the initial phase in a flow rate of greater than 0.7 m / sec and up to 2.0 m / sec and b) in the subsequent phase in a flow rate speed of 0.3 m/ sec to less than 0.7 m / sec performed, wherein the flow velocity is each relating to gas-free electrolytes.
