method of tanning rawhide.

<ABSTRACT>
a method of tanning rawhide by pickling the rawhide  after the preparatory operation has been finished, and then  treating the pickled rawhide with chromium salt to thereby  prepare leather.  according to this invention, the pickling pro­ cess is performed in the presence of urotropin (hex­ amethylene tetramine), whereby the ph value in a tanning  bath of chromium salt may be easily adjusted, the coupling of  rawhide with basic complex salt of chromium may be  increased, and the amount of the residual chromium in the  waste liquid may be minimized.  further, in order to improve  the operation efficiency in the finishing step, to further  enhance the quality of the leather, and to render the  chromium content in the waste liquid almost zero, the tanned  leather is treated with ammonia or weakly acidic ammonium  salt in the used chromium bath.  
</ABSTRACT>
