<ABSTRACT>
Method for producing low molecular weight polyhydroxyl compounds by condensation of formaldehyde hydrate in the presence of calcium hydroxide as a catalyst as well as terminated for endioluke compounds as a cocatalyst, characterized in that a formaldehyde-containing solution of the cocatalyst in water and optionally low molecular weight or polyhydric alcohols and / or higher molecular weights Polyhydroxyl compounds at a temperature of 80 to 110 ° C by addition of calcium hydroxide to a pH of 9 to 12, so that the condensation of the formaldehyde hydrate is started, then a 20 to 65 wt. % formaldehyde-containing aqueous formalin solution and / or paraformaldehyde dispersion and calcium hydroxide so that the reaction mixture is maintained at a temperature of 80 to 110 ° C and in a pH range between 7.5 and 9.5, while during the whole course The condensation reaction the formaldehyde concentration between 0.5 and 10 wt. %, is kept based on total reaction mixture and finally, if appropriate, the residual amount of 0.5 to 10 wt. % Formaldehyde is removed by further condensation at pH values ​​below 7 or reactions reactive with other compounds other than formaldehyde hydrate.
