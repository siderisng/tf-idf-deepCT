<TITLE>
process for the preparation of copolymers as viscosity increasing agents.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
1.  a process for the production of a copolymer based on methacrylic acid, a conjugated diolefin and the ester of an alpha-beta-ethylenically unsaturated dicarbonic acid at elevated temperature as viscosity increasing agent, comprising copolymerization of 50 to 60 wt.  % of methacrylic acid, 15 to 49 wt.  % of a conjugated diolefin and 1 to 25 wt.  % of fumaric acid dialkyl ester and/or maleic acid dialkyl ester with 1 to 8 c-atoms in the alcohol component in aqueous emulsion at a phase ratio monomer mixture : water of 1:1. 7 to 1:2. 5 at temperatures in the range of 35 degrees c to 60 degrees c, whereby - related to the monomer content - the aqueous phase contains 0. 02 to 1 wt.  % of a basic compound, 0. 05 to 1 wt.  % of an initiator, 0. 01 to 1 wt.  % of a modifier as well as 0. 1 to 5 wt.  % of an emulsifier. 
</ABSTRACT>
