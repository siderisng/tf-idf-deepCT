<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to wood protection agent-containing air-crosslinking paint binders based on copolymers of copolymerized units of glycidyl (meth) acrylates and at least one further vinyl monomer, wherein the copolymer - always relative to paint binder - with 0.01 to 30 wt. % of at least one wood protection agent with at least one hydrogen atom responsive over the glycidyl groups and 20 to 60 wt. % air-drying natural or isomerized fatty acids or mixtures and with 0 to 10 wt. % of a dicarboxylic anhydride has been reacted, and the paint binder has average molecular weights Mn of 1500 to 15,000 and acid numbers from 3 to 15. A particular advantage of the paint binders according to the invention is the chemical binding of the wood protection agent to the polymer molecule, whereby a delayed release of the active ingredient takes place and thus a prolonged protective effect is achieved.
