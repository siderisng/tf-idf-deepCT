<ABSTRACT>
The present invention relates to a method for producing adhesion-reducing coatings to thin-film substrates, in particular on paper, by applying a solvent-containing mixture consisting of (a) an α, ω-dihydroxypolydimethylsiloxane (b) a polyorganosiloxane end-blocked with trimethylsiloxy groups Si-bonded hydrogen-having polyorganosiloxane and (c) a heavy metal carboxylate or dialkyltin carboxylate, wherein the mixture is added to the mixture prior to curing at higher temperature (D) a disilamoropholinsiloxane obtained by reacting a chloromethyl polymethylsiloxane with maximum equivalent amounts of a primary amine in the presence of a proton catcher.
</ABSTRACT>
