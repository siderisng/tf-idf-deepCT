<ABSTRACT>
an n-substituted amide of a lower aliphatic acid, prefer­ ably n,n-dimethylformamide, or a solvent mixture including  such an amide, is used as the solvent for the reaction of a  2,4-diaminophenyl ether with an acylating agent to produce  the corresponding 2-amino-4-acylaminophenyl ether.  such a  solvent or solvent mixture may also be employed as the  reduction solvent for the catalytic reduction of a 2,4-­ dinitrophenyl ether to the corresponding 2,4-diaminophenyl  ether.  the hydrogenation reaction product mixture can be  employed directly in the above-mentioned acylation reaction,  without concentration or separation of the 2,4-diaminophenyl  ether.  the reduction and acylation processes according to the  invention give a better selectivity and yield of the required  products than the corresponding conventional processes  using other solvents.  
</ABSTRACT>
