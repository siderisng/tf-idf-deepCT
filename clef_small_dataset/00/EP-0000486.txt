<ABSTRACT>
stabilized carrier-bonded proteins are achieved by reacting a protein in aqueous solution with a water-insoluble, female-free carrier material containing proteins binding active groups, and by subsequent action of hydrogen sulfide or from preferably aliphatic compounds having a molecular weight under 5000, preferably under 2000, with at least obtain a composition group and at least one further feature, hydroxy or primary or secondary amino group. Preferably, a perl-shaped carrier material having a pearl diameter between 5 and 1000 μm based on a crosslinked polyacrylamide with oxirane groups or cyanobromized cellulose, agarose or dextrans with an enzyme, e.g. b. Penicillinacylase reacted. Subsequently, at least 0.03 wt. -% of free movement groups attached to the carrier material. The products are characterized by reduced dismantling of biological activity with multiple reuse of the bonded protein.
