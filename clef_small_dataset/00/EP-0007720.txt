<ABSTRACT>
an electrically operated line monitor for tennis which  uses one or more rays (3², 3³, 3⁴) substantially smaller in  cross-section than a tennis ball to monitor areas of a tennis  court (6¹, 6², 6³) adjacent to the critical lines (24).  the rays (3²,  3³, 3⁴) pass over the playing surface of the court (6¹, 6², 6³) at a  height lower than the height of a tennis ball and when a  plurality of rays (3², 3³, 3⁴) are employed they are spaced apart  so that a ball in play striking the ground (6¹, 6², 6³) in a  monitored area will interrupt the passage of at least one of the  rays (3², 3³, 3⁴), thereby actuating a signal.  if the rays (3³, 3⁴)  are monitoring an area (6², 6¹) just outside a critical line (24),  i. e.  an area (6², 6¹) in which a ball striking in it would be a  'fault', the signal can be an alarm and an additional indepen­ dant master ray (3²) may be provided to monitor the area (6³,  6²) just inside the critical line (24) and which, if its is inter­ rupted by the passage of a ball, negatives any subsequent  signal.  
</ABSTRACT>
