a substrate (1) is made for silicon solar cells by heating a  sheet of large-grained silicon steel at a temperature of at least  about 1300°c in an atmosphere of hydrogen and tungsten  hexafluoride (or hexachloride) at a partial pressure ratio of hy­ drogen to tungsten hexafluoride of about 3 to about 6 to de­ posit an epitaxial layer of tungsten (3) on said sheet of silicon  steel.  epitaxial silicon (4) can then be deposited in a conven­ tional manner on the layer of epitaxial tungsten.  with such substrate used, diffusion of iron into the silicon  layer which would otherwise make the cell ineffective is mini­ mized.
