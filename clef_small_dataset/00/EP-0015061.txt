image stabilized optical system.

<ABSTRACT>
a telescopic optical system has means for stabilising the  position of an image relative to a reticle, e. g.  when the system  is subject to vibration.  the system comprises an objective (1a), an eyepiece (2a),  an erect prism (3a) located between the objective (1a) and the  eyepiece (2a), and a reticle (11) located on the focal plane of  the objective (1a).  the objective (1a), eyepiece (2a) and reticle  (11) are fixed to a casing of the system.  the erect prism (3a) is  rotatably mounted on the casing by means of gimbals pro­ vided with a rotor.  the gimbals have two axes of rotation  (6-6′, 7-7′) intersecting at right angles with each other and  included in a plane which is perpendicular to the main optical  axis (4a) of the telescopic optical system and passes through  middle point (a) of the distance from the objective to the  reticle.  in a binocular system two optical systems have parallel  main optical axes (4a, 4b) but only one reticle (11) need be  provided with a scale (12).  
</ABSTRACT>
