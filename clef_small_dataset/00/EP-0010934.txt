<ABSTRACT>
an information handling system comprising a plurality  of interconnected cells (1400, 1500) is capable of directly  and efficiently executing a predetermined class of pro­ gramming languages known as applicative languages.  the  system may include two interconnected networks of cells,  one of which may be a linear array of cells and the other  of which may be a tree networks of cells.  the system is  able directly to interpret a high level, applicative language  and is capable of operating on an wide range of classes of  programs.  within practical limits, the system accommoda­ tes the unbounded parallelism permitted by applicative  languages in a single user program.  the system also has  the capability of executing many user programs simultan­ eously.  
</ABSTRACT>
