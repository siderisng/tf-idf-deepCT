<ABSTRACT>
a process for preparing m-(p-bromophenoxy)-benzaldehyde  of the formula:    is characterized by brominating m-phenoxybenzaldehyde  with bromine or bromine chloride as a brominating agent.  this process, achieving a nucleic halogenation is a  nucleus of an aldehyde which nucleus is not aldehyde-substituted,  is preferably carried out in a halogenation-resistant  solvent and may be carried out with heating.  if a  solvent is employed, however, the reaction temperature is  preferably below the boiling point of the solvent.  the presence of a catalyst is not essential to the  performance of the process of the invention.
