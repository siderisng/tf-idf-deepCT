<ABSTRACT>
Method and apparatus for exposure by corpuscular beams - shadow thread in which the patterns to be exposed are generated by two or more sub-expansions by means of a corresponding number of complementary masks (7a, 7b). The relative shift between the mask (7) and the object (8) required before each sub-exposure (8) is composed of a rough adjustment by a physical shift, the deviation of which is measured by the target setting interferometrically, and a subsequent electronic fine adjustment by a Swiveling of the mask enforcing body cypine beam (10a, 10b) by a point approximately in the mask plane takes place. By this pivot about a angle defined as a function of the determined alignment error (α1, α2), a displacement of the silhouette of the mask occurs on the object to be exposed, the amount of which the amount of the alignment error is equal to the direction of the direction of the alignment error.
