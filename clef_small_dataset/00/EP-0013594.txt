<ABSTRACT>
the rheological properties of an extrudable material can  be measured by extruding a strand of the material, measuring  its diameter continuously, to give a series of diameter values,  interpreting the variations in the strand diameter so as to  characterize the surface of the strand, and deriving rheologi足 cal data from the surface characteristics of the strand.  the  variations in the strand diameter can be interpreted as to  peak-to-peak amplitude, average diameter value, the fre足 quency of significant extreme values, standard deviations  from a mean value, and the like.  by means of these mathemat足 ical characterizations, the rheological behavior of the extrud足 able material can then be derived.  the scorch behavior of a  curable elastomer can be measured.  the heterogeneity of a  rubber compound can be described, or the macromolecular  properties of a polymer can be measured (such as "gel", or  degree of cross-linking. )
