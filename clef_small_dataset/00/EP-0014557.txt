<ABSTRACT>
metal values, such as iron, may be stripped from an  organic liquid medium loaded with metal ions by a process  which comprises contacting the organic medium with an  aqueous phase at a contact temperature which is sufficiently  high to cause the metal values to precipitate as metal oxide,  hydroxide or hydrate (or a mixture thereof) in the presence of  the organic medium.
