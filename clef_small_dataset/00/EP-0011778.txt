<ABSTRACT>
The invention consists of a collective fountain or container (2) for liquid. The lower part (3) of the container (2) extends a riser (5) which has a certain slope compared to the horizontal. This line extends via a manifold (6) and any rejuvenation (8) into a vertical dropping pipe (7), which is connected to a mainly U-shaped tube (9) with horizontal outflow (10). When the container (2) is filled with liquid and fused white liquid with a throughput which exceeds the minimum throughput, which can flow continuously through the device, forms a liquid fitting closure in the tube (9). The fact that liquid flows from this into the outflow (10), a negative pressure in the vertical trap pipe (7), which looks at the container (2), resulting in a lifting effect. The liquid leaves the Vorrich device in the form of contiguous stuffing with great kinetic energy. The device allows thin lines in wastewater systems to be used, without the required self-cleaning capability is lost, even if low-consumed closettes are connected to the systems. Further applications are artificial irrigation and batchwise dosage of liquid.
