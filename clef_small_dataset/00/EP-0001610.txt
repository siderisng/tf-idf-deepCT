<ABSTRACT>
a method of producing a moulding having a plastics covering and composed of a water-containing mass of fibrous material impregnated with natural and/or synthetic resin material and adjusted to acidity, the mass being composed of fibrous materials based on cellulose fibres, wood fibres, wood pulp, paper fibres, wool fibres, cotton fibres, hairs, synthetic fibres, glass fibres, asbestos fibres, or mixtures thereof, as a rough pressing or moulding blank in woven and/or non-woven form, comprises the steps of coating the rough pressing or moulding blank by a wet-on-wet process with an aqueous dispersion, emulsion or solution adjusted to alkalinity of a polyurethane resin binder capable of forming a thermosetting film, applying a plastics covering consisting of a plastics film of polyvinyl chloride, polyvinylidene chloride, polyethylene, polypropylene, or copolymers of polyethylene and polypropylene to the rough pressing or moulding blank, removing water by vacuum from the mass of fibrous material, and shaping and curing the resin material by hot pressing. 
</ABSTRACT>
