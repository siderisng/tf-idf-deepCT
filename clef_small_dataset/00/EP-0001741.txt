<ABSTRACT>
The counting of thrombocytes in blood samples after a counting chamber method under the microscope is difficult in the presence of erythrocytes.By adding 20 μ ~ L of the blood to be examined to 2 ml of an aqueous solution of mercury II chloride in a concentration between 1.2 and 4.0 g / l or better between 1.5 and 3.5 g / lwhose pH was set between 1.5 and 4.8, better still between 2.0 and 4.5, the erythrocytes are decolorized and the thrombocytes are lit in the microscopic image, so that the counting is considerably facilitated.The pH is expediently adjusted by adding hydrochloric acid, nitric acid or mercury II nitrate.
