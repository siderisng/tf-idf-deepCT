<ABSTRACT>
Method for producing lateral PNP or NPN transistors in isolated, monocrystalline silicon regions isolated by silica insulating zones, wherein the PNP or NPN transistors are formed partially below the surface of the semiconductor within the insulated monocrystalline zone. The p-emitters or N-emitter diffusion is performed over the portion of the silica layer which extends partially into the insulated monocrystalline zone. Through this structure, the vertical current injection is reduced, so that a relatively high (beta) gain is obtained even with small base emitter voltages. The lateral PNP or NPN transistor produced by this method lies in an isolated monocrystalline silicon zone, wherein the silica insulation surrounds the zone and partially surrounded below the surface into the insulated monocrystalline silicon zone. The p-emitter or N-emitter diffusion lies above the portion of the silica layer, which partially extends into the monocrystalline-insulated zone.
