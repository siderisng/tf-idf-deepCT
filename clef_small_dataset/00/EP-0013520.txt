<ABSTRACT>
a process for producing a chromium-silicon silicide barrier for a schottky diode permitting an adjustment of the penetration (b) of the barrier below the surface by controlling the thickness (c) of a chromium layer (14) deposited on a silicon block (10) carrying an epitaxially grown layer (11).  the chromium vaporised under vacuum is entirely combined with the silicon in the course of a heat treatment at a temperature within the range between 600 deg c and 700 deg c.  this process permits the production of power diodes of differing characteristics by modification of the thickness of the chromium layer.  <image>
