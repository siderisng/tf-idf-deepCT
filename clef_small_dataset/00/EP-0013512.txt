<ABSTRACT>
a method of preparing a water-soluble carboxylakylated  chitin comprises   (i) freezing a highly concentrated aqueous alkali solution  which contains chitin; and   (ii) dispersing the frozen mixture obtained from step (i) in  an organic solvent containing an etherifying agent of formula:   x(ch₂)ncooh   wherein x represents chlorine or bromine, and n denotes an  interger of 1, 2 or 3, thereby to etherify hydroxyl groups of the  chitin.  the carboxyalkylated chitin may be deacetylated by treat­ ing it with an aqueous alkali.  the carboxyalkylated and de­ acetylated carboxyalkylated chitin derivatives can be used in  techniques of ion-exchange.  
</ABSTRACT>
