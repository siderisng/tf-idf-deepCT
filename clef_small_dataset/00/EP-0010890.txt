<ABSTRACT>
automatic tool changers for machine tools having  spindle mechanisms which can be driven selectively in op­ posite directions along the spindle have previously relied  on sensing switches to detect spindle mechanism position  and to control a spindle mechanism drive.  the use of  sensing switches has caused problems in attaining smooth  control and in reliability.  this invention uses a position signal generator (31)  arranged to provide position signals whenever a rotary  spindle mechanism drive (8, 9) rotates, and an arrange­ ment for computing the position of the spindle me­ chanism (7) on the basis of the position signals and for  varying the speed of the spindle mechanism in dependen­ ce upon the computed position.  no sensing switches need  be used.  this invention can be used in an automatic tool chan­ ger for a vertical machining center.
