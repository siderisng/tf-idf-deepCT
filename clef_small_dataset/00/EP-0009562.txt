<ABSTRACT>
In the exposure of semiconductor plates covered with a photoresist layer for the production of integrated semiconductor circuits, the surface to be exposed is contaminated by a corpuscular beam, whose cross-section is much smaller than this surface. The locally different alignment errors obtained by translation, especially by rotation of the mask with respect to the semiconductor tape to be exposed to be discharged, which is determined by appropriate measurement methods and compensated by distinctly different automatically adjusted tilting of the body cell beam. If, for example, in precisely aligned agents, points of mask and semiconductor plates are presented by an alignment error due to a slight angular rotation of the mask with respect to the semiconductor tape, the local errors are linear functions of the distances of the respective area from the center. These errors can be calculated in a simple manner with knowledge of the angular rotation and to be pensed by appropriate areas of different tilting of the corpuscular beam at the exposure.
