<TITLE>
chopper circuit.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>

</INVENTOR>
<ABSTRACT>
call devices in telephone installations are often operated by means of a sinusoidal pulsating d. c.  voltage.  a chopper circuit is specified which generates a d. c.  voltage that constantly changes the instantaneous values, it being possible for the peak value of the d. c.  voltage to be higher than the supply voltage.  this chopper circuit has an inductor (ue), which can be connected to the input (e) of the chopper circuit via a first switch (s1), bridged by a diode (d1), and can be connected to a capacitor (c) via a second switch (s2) bridged by a diode (d2).  the two switches (s1, s2) are controlled in accordance with the isolating transformer principle such that the energy can flow either from the input (e) to the capacitor (c) or from the capacitor (c) to the input (e). 
</ABSTRACT>
