<ABSTRACT>
a non-occlusive, non-adherent dressing for wounds, the  dressing comprising an imperforate (i. e.  continuous) flexible film  (10, 12, 14) for contacting the wound, and an absorbent pad (16,  18) backing the wound-contacting film (14) and extending be­ yond a substantial portion of the periphery (20) of the film (10,  12, 14), the dressing having vapor transport capacity appro­ ximating that of intact human skin.  the absorpent pad (16, 18)  removes wound exudate from the proximate area of the wound  without the pad (16, 18) coming into contact with the open  wound itseff.  the final construction, comprising the film (10, 12,  14) and absorbent backing (16, 18), permits a measurable  amount of moisture vapor to be transmitted (greater than 0. 06  mg/cm²/hr), thereby greatly reducing the degree of maceration  and other problems that may be produced by a fully occlusive  dressing.
