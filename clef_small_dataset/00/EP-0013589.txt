<ABSTRACT>
bonded nonwoven fabrics are softened by impinging the  fabrics with a fluid jet having jet characteristics correlated to  effect at least a twenty-five percent reduction in bending  modulus of the fabric.  the softening effect can be increased  by wetting the fabric prior to jet impingement.
