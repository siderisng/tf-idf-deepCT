<ABSTRACT>
Method for producing polymalic acids having molar weights between 200 and 1500 by polymerization of maleic anhydride in the presence of hydrogen peroxide and acetic anhydride and saponification of the polymalic anhydride formed, characterized in that the maleic anhydride is dissolved in the 1 to 5 times molar amount of acetic anhydride with the 0.2-to 0.5 times molar amount of hydrogen peroxide with simultaneous heating of the reaction mixture to 80 to 140 ° C and after the addition of the hydrogen peroxide, the reaction leads to 100 to 140 ° C to an end.The method can advantageously be carried out in the presence of non-oxidizing acids, reducing agents and, preferred, of reducing acids such as phosphorous acids or oxalic acid.The obtained polymalic acids are suitable as a fuel substances and incrusting inhibitors.
</ABSTRACT>
