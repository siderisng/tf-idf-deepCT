<ABSTRACT>
On a n-doped Si layer (2) on an electrically conductive layer, aluminum and silicon is destroyed by vacuum evaporation, cathode attrition and the like.This multilayer body is then subjected to a heating process by applying a temperature below the eutectic point of the aluminum silicon melt (577 ° C).During this heating, a dense network of P-doped silicon pyramids (7) forms on the silicon layer (2) which hereby form a PN junction 3.Above the silicon pyramids (7), an aluminum thin film (5) spreads out, which is used as a counter electrode (50) to said electrical conductive layer (1).
