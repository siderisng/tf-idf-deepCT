<TITLE>
process for the treatment of sugar solutions using ion exchange resins.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>
rohm & haasus<sep>rohm and haas company<sep>rohm and haas companyindependence mall westphiladelphia pennsylvania 19105us<sep>rohm and haas company<sep>
</APPLICANT>
<INVENTOR>
cartier peter glenn<sep>cartier, peter glenn<sep>cartier, peter glenn13 chelfield roadglenside, pa 19038us<sep>cartier, peter glenn<sep>cartier, peter glenn13 chelfield roadglenside, pa 19038us<sep>
</INVENTOR>
<ABSTRACT>
sugar solutions may be treated with fine particles of  ion exchange resin for example to decolorise and clarify  the solutions in the case of anion exchange resins and to  invert the sugar or soften the solutions in the case of  cation exchange resins.  the resins comprise approximately  spherical beads of crosslinked copolymer having diameters  of 0. 01 to 1. 5 micrometers and bearing 0. 7 to 1. 5 ion  exchange functional groups per monomer unit.  the solu­ tions may contain ionically charged particulate impurities  which will form a floc with the ion exchange resin, particu­ larly anion exchange resin, or a separate flocculating agent  may be added and the floc subsequently separated from  the sugar solution.  
</ABSTRACT>
