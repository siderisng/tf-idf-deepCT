<ABSTRACT>
In order to produce a hardened photoresist mask, a photoresist material (14) is deposited on an oxide covered or bare substrate (2) which serves as a main mask for selective blocking of certain areas.The photoresist layer (14) is chemically, thermally cured by ion implantation or reactive plasma technique after a desired main pattern was formed in it.After the patterns of the cutouts are formed, the photoresist layer is cured so that it becomes insoluble.On the photoresist layer (14), a selectively blocking layer of conventional photomaterials for forming blocks (26) and (28) can be applied, whereby in subsequent ion implantation in certain cutouts (eg 18 and 22), implantation is prevented and in other cutouts(eg 16, 20 and 24) the implantation can be performed.
