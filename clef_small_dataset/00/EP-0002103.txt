<ABSTRACT>
a blood cell counter has sensing electrodes (12 and  16) which detect the passage of a cell through an aperture  (80).  a purge system serves to purge the aperture (80) and  the surrounding portions of the blood cell counter of dirt,  debris, and previous sample liquid.  a sample tube (10)  extends from a source of either sample liquid or cleansing  solution to a small gap (35) between the aperture (80) and  a housing (40).  a purge line (34) is connected between an  annulus (33) surrounding the gap (35) and a pump (86)  which supplies negative pressure.  due to the geometry  of the sample tube (10), gap (35), annulus (33) and purge  line (34), a high pressure drop occurs across the gap (35)  and the liquid flows ar relatively high velocity past the  aperture (80) to facilitate cleaning.
