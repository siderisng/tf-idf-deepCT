<TITLE>
use of a soot concentrate based on an ethylene copolymer for the manufacture of shaped polyethylene bodies for tubes
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
the use of a homogeneous mixture comprising from 70 to 95% by weight of high-pressure polyethylene and from 5 to 30% by weight of a carbon black concentrate which itself comprises from 50 to 95% by weight of an ethylene copolymer with copolymerised monoethylenically unsaturated carboxylic acid esters, such as an ethylene-vinyl acetate copolymer, and from 5 to 50% by weight of carbon black having a particle size of from 5 to 70  mu m, for the production of tubes.  the carbon black concentrate may be stabilised in the conventional manner.  the advantages achieved by means of the invention are, in particular, that the tubes produced from the mixture have a creep rupture strength which is from three to six times higher than that for tubes produced from moulding compounds coloured using carbon black concentrates comprising polyethylene and carbon black. 
</ABSTRACT>
