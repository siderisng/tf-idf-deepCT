<ABSTRACT>
In a method for producing a composite mat consisting of mineral fibers and metal fibers, preferably basal wool and stainless steel wool, in particular for use as heat and corrosion resistant material for the sound-absorbing filling of silencers on internal combustion engines, the metal fibers (1) are at or immediately after the storage of the mineral fibers ( 3) to the mat combined with the mineral fibers, wherein the mineral fibers may have a maximum binder content (2) of 1%. For producing thicker composite mats, the binder is introduced into the mineral fiber mat so that its proportion shifts from one mat side to another as a function of thickness and density of the mineral fiber mat. In a device for carrying out these methods, at least one fiber assembly for producing the mineral fiber mat and a dry tunnel is provided, which is passed through the mineral fiber mat coming from the mating aggregate mineral fiber mat with a binder content of a maximum of 1%, wherein for the mineral fibers, an additional insertion device between the fiber assembly and dry tunnel provided is. The nozzles for feeding the binder may be controllable or have different nozzle performance.
</ABSTRACT>
