<ABSTRACT>
Method for obtaining aromatic n-hexane from a mixture of aromatics and non-aromatics by extractive distillation. The mixture is transferred to a first distillation column, distilled and a benzene of relapsed marsh product and the non-aromatic-centered head product. The distillate is subtracted at the top of the feeding of the mixture to be processed since Lich and transferred to the upper part of a second DE Stillierkolonne. From this second Destillierko Lonne, the hexane section is pulled off as a marsh product and gives it approximately in the middle of an extractive distillation column. Then, it is extracted with a selective solvent which is abandoned above the supply of the sump product of the second Destil Lierkolonne which is cut out of the hexane. The selective solvent-containing marsh product is withdrawn and the head-led vapors after condensation as aromatics free N-hexane.
