<ABSTRACT>
The irradiated fuels are dissolved in a solution of nitric acid, the vapors constituted essentially of the oxides of nitrogen, iodine and water formed during the dissolution in a condenser 2, then in a first column.absorption 3 where the recombinant nitric acid is formed.The recombinant acid is returned to the solver 1 while the leaving gases of said column are sent to a second absorption column 6 countercurrent of an alkaline solution which is loaded with iodine and ions and the alkaline solution.The second absorption column is sent to a reactor 8 containing a mixture of nitric acid and sulfamic acid which destroys nitrous products and releases iodine.Nuclear applications.
