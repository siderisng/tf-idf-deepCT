<ABSTRACT>
a process for removing dinitrogen difluoride from an  atmosphere containing nitrogen trifluoride comprises bringing  the atmosphere into contact with a metal capable of  defluorinating dinitrogen difluoride but inert to nitrogen  trifluoride and maintaining the metal and the atmosphere in  contact at a sufficient temperature and for a sufficient time to  effect the required degrees of defluorination of the dinitrogen  difluoride.  preferably, the metal is selected from the group consisting  of iron, cobalt and nickel and the temperature is between  300 and 1,000Â°f.  the process is limited to atmospheres where the  concentration of dinitrogen difluoride does not exceed 5% by  volume.
