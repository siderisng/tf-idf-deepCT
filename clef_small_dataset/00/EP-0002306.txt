<ABSTRACT>
To obtain coal in situ, a borehole (23) is brought out of the day surface, which extends at least to a large part in the area of the flozes (26), provided that the conditions allow the conditions.At the end or near the end of this hole, the hole is extended over such a length and such a width, as a minimum twice the expected deflection in the horizontal projection of a second well (31) which is drilled up to this extension (30), corresponds.The extension is preferably brought about mechanically path.After the holes are connected to each other, the coal is recovered by gasification, dissolution or extraction, possibly under supercritical conditions and, if necessary, in combination with chemical / physical powder.The inventive method is particularly suitable for the 'inline' gasification method.
