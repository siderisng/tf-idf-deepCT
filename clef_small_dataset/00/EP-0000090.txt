<ABSTRACT>
modified phenolic resins are prepared by condensing  approximately equimolar quantities of a phenolic component  comprising one or more phenols, and a diamino component  comprising one or more di-(secondary amino) compounds  with approximately twice the molar quantity of formaldehyde  under mannich reaction conditions.  mercury and  copper ions may be removed very selectively from aqueous  solutions using the new resin.
