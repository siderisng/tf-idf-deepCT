<ABSTRACT>
a process for making a high molecular weight  polyamide, wherein a diacid-rich component and a diamine-rich  component are prepared separately in nonstoichiometric  proportions.  each of the separately prepared components  melts below the melting temperature of the polyamide product,  and preferably below 200Â°c.  the diacid-rich component  and the diamine-rich component are contacted in the liquid  state at a high enough temperature to prevent solidification,  and in proportions such that the total amounts of diacid and  diamine are as close as possible to stoichiometric.  a major  utility of this process is in the production of nylon 66, a known  fiber-forming polyamide and engineering plastic.
