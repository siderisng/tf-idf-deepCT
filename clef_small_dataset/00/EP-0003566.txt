<ABSTRACT>
the level indicator is preferably suitable for fuel tanks.  measurement of the level (3) in a container (1) is undertaken by means of a fracture-resistant rod (4) with light-conducting properties, which rod has a light emitter (5) and a light receiver (6) respectively at its two ends and is resistant to conventional fuels.  the light flux flowing through the rod (4) is influenced by the level (3), so that the electric current emitted by the light receiver (6) is employed as a measure of the level (fig.  2).  <image>
