<ABSTRACT>
the invention relates to a process for selectively removing  minor amounts of beta-chloroacroleins and 5,6-epoxyhexene-1  from a mixture made up predominantly of  epichlorohydrin, characterized by treating said mixture with  hydrogen at a temperature of between 20°c and 150°c in the  presence of a catalyst comprising rhodium, platinum or palladium  deposited on a non-acidic, refractory support, in an  amount of from 0. 05 to 10% by weight on the total catalyst and  to epichlorohydrin whenever prepared by means of this process.
