<ABSTRACT>
Process for the preparation of lactosylure and its N-methyl derivative, dietary supplements for ruminants.According to this process, lactose and urea are heated at 70-110 ° with a minimum of water and, as much as possible, this water is eliminated by evaporation during the reaction.The crude reaction product may be purified by trituration with iced water or ethanol and spraying so as to remove by dissolution in these solvents, the unsuccessable urea.The corresponding N-methylol derivative is obtained by reaction with formaldehyde.
