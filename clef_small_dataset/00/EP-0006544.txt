<ABSTRACT>
The implantable bone replacement materials Best from a massive core (1) of tissue contracts chem metal, such as titanium, and as a contact surface with the body tissue, have a marginal zone of the metal into which a single-layered layer of coarse particulate calciumphous phat ceramic (2) is incorporated. The ceramic particles (2) are open at the surface of the implant. Additionally or instead of the coarse particle ceramic can be stored in the edge zone or evenly distributed in the entire material fine particle ceramic. The edge zone can also be designed as a sprayed, multilayer cermetic layer layer. The deposits (2) are bioac tive, sintered, completely or partially resorbable calcium phosphate ceramic. For the production of the bone replacement materials z. b. Spherical calcium phosphate particles glued to the inner wall of a mold and then the mold is poured out with the metal. The matrix material can also be added as a powder into the mold, which then closes a hot squeezing or isostatic hot pressing. In other cases, the coating is applied to the metal core by flame or plasma spraying.
