<ABSTRACT>
In an electrostatic pressure or copying machine, a charge image of a form to be printed on a photoelectric recording medium is generated using the form pressure device. In this case, the form pressure device consists of a roller (WA) with a transparent cylinder (LY) disposed between two roller receiving flanges (FL1, FL2) and a lamp tube (LR) arranged in the roller. On the transparent cylinder (LY), the print template (FO) is deposited. The printing template is illuminated with the aid of light emanating from the lamp tube and mapped on the recording medium. For easy replacement of the roller (WA), the one drum receiving flange (FL1) is provided at its circumference with a groove (Nu) and a toothing (Va). To attach the roller (WA) in the printer, the roller is pushed onto the lamp tube (LR). Subsequently, the lamp tube (LR) is pivoted with the roller (WA) on a housing part (GH). In this case, the centering roll (Zt) engages in the groove (Nu) of the roll receiving flange (FL1) and brings the roller (WA) in the correct position. Subsequently, the drive gear (at) engages in the toothing (Va) of the one roll receiving flange (FL1). The centering roller (ZT) brought the roller (WA) in the correct position and locked there.
