method for the fabrication of plastic tubes serving as drainage probes for wounds, in particular tubes provided with an antithrombogenic skin, and plastic tubes obtained.

<ABSTRACT>
plastic tubes intended for use as drainage probes for wounds are prepared by taking a plastic tube (1), provided with drainage holes (2) prepared by punching, and immersing the tube in an organic solvent in order to remove the surface irregularities (3) which occur during punching and which project into the lumen of the holes and of the tube and cause secretion obstructions and/or thrombocyte aggregations, leaving the tube in the solvent for a few minutes, and then taking the tube out and immediately removing the solvent by drying.  in this way, plastic tubes are obtained in which the edges of the punched holes (2) are smooth and free from irregularities, such as fringes or tabs, so that the use of these plastic tubes as drainage probes for wounds does not lead to undesired secretion obstructions and/or thrombocyte aggregations impairing the healing of the wound.  if the tubes produced in accordance with the described procedures are to be additionally provided with an antithrombogenic skin, they undergo a subsequent treatment with a 1% strength solution of polyethylene-acrylate or with a mixture of tetrahydrofuran and toluene, by which means there is formed on the surface of the tubes a thin layer with a negative charge, which repels the thrombocytes and their aggregates.  <image>
