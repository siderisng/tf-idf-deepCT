<ABSTRACT>
Process for preparing detergents-dispersants metal liquors. 1) By reacting from a sulfur on a alkylphenol carrying one or more alkyl substituents in C₆-C₆₀, in the presence of a dilution oil, an alkylbenzene alkaline earth metal sulfonate of molecular weight greater than 300 and of TBN less than or equal to 150, an alkaline earth compound, possibly an alkali metal hydroxide, a tank foot based on a TBN metal detergent greater than or equal to 200 selected from alkylphenates Linnait Suraca and sulfurized alkaline earth metal, alkaline earth alkaline earth alkyl-metal sulfonates and alkaline earth alkaline earth and alkylbenzene metal alkaline earth alkaline earth metal mixtures, and alkaline earth metal sulfonates, and alkylene glycol at a temperature between 100 and 190 ° C. 2) carbonating the mixture obtained with carbon dioxide at a temperature of between 100 and 200 ° C, the amount of CO₂ being between that which can be completely absorbed and an excess of 30% of this quantity. 3) by eliminating alkylene glycol. 4) by separating the high alkalinity metal dispersant thus obtained. Application of dispersants thus obtained to improve the detergent-dispersing power of lubricating oils.
