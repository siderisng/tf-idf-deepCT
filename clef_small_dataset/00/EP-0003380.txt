<ABSTRACT>
a process for the manufacture of a mixture of 3-phenoxyl-benzyl  bromide and 3-phenoxybenzal bromide by  the reaction of a molar excess of molecular bromine on  3-phenoxytoluene, wherein the molar excess of bromine is in  the range 1. 3 to 1. 5 moles of molecular bromine per mol.  of  the toluene, and in that the reaction is carried out at a  temperature in the range 195°c to 235°c in the presence of an  inert gas and in the absence of: (a) actinic radiation; (b) an  added catalyst or initiator; or (c) an added solvent.  the  mixture of bromides is a useful starting material in the  manufacture of 3-phenoxybenzaldehyde which is a key  intermediate in the manufacture of pyrethroid insecticides.  
</ABSTRACT>
