<ABSTRACT>
The invention is a method for coating glass surfaces, in particular glassfla, for preventing the scattering of glass particles during bursting, with a transparent, thermosetting protection layer of more than 50 microns. In this case, the pure glass surface is first treated with an aqueous or alcoholic silane simply and then dried. Subsequently, a mass of aliphatically and / or cycloaliphatic diisocyanates or its reaction products with water in the molar ratio of 2: ≦ and a mixture of hydroxyl-containing compounds and, if necessary, if necessary, if applicable under heat supply, is applied to the thus-treated glass surface. The latter mixture consists of: 40 - 80 wt. % trifunctional, hydroxyl group-coated polyesters made of ω-hydroxycarboxylic acids or their lactones with at least 4 C atoms and a mean molecular weight of 300-1400, 15-50 wt. % trifunctional, hydroxyl group-alti metal polyesters made of ω-hydroxycarboxylic acids or their lactones with at least 4 C atoms and a mean molecular weight of 400-2000 and 2.5 - 20 wt. % Aliphatic and / or cycloaliphatic diols and, where appropriate, conventional processing aids. The invention also relates to the coated with a transparent, thermoset protective layer glass bottles.
