<ABSTRACT>
the invention is relating to sodium amoxicillin prepara­ tions which may easily be converted into stable, suitably  injectable solutions which are free of allergic reactions caus­ ing chemicals and which are containing besides sodium  amoxicillin, a small amount of sodium hydroxide and option­ ally also a small amount of sodium chloride, to a process for  their preparation and to a process for the preparation of aque­ ous sodium amoxicillin containing injection solutions.  the sodium amoxicillin preparations are prepared by the  as fast but gradual addition as possible of an excess sodium  hydroxide to a suspension of amoxicillin trihydrate in water,  until the amoxicillin has completely dissolved, whereafter  optionally a part of the excess of base is immediately neutral­ ized with hydrochloric acid, whereby the temperature is kept  between 0-30°c, followed by sterile filtration of the obtained  solution, freezing the solution and freeze drying the obtained  solid in flasks or in bulk.
