a process for preparing a stable, high surface alpha-alumina from gamma-alumina.

<ABSTRACT>
in a process for preparing a stable high surface alpha-­ alumina from gamma-alumina a gamma-alumina having  pores of average size in the range 20 to 100 å is impregnated  with a carbonizable carbonaceous material such as a sugar.   the impregnated gamma-alumina is heated under non-­ oxidizing conditions to convert the carbonaceous material to  carbon.  the resulting carbon-containing gamma-alumina is  then further heated under non-oxidizing conditions to convert  the gamma-alumina to alpha-alumina, and finally the  carbon-containing alpha-alumina is heated in an oxidizing  atmosphere to remove the carbon by oxidation.  the product  is an alpha-alumina having a relatively high surface area and  which can withstand temperatures of at least 1000°c in the  presence of steam without a substantial loss of area.  the  alpha-alumina product is useful as a catalyst or catalyst sup­ port in, e. g.  petroleum refining processes.
