optical fibre connector.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
an optical fibre (116) is received in an interstatial  space between two converging alignment surfaces (46,  24) of a connector body (16).  an extension (42) of the  first surface (46) projects beyond the second surface (24)  so that, on mating with a similar connector body (16),  each extension (42) retains an optical fibre (116) in the  interstatial space of the other body (16) pressing the op­ tical fibre (116) against the first and second alignment  surfaces (46, 24) of the other body (16) precisely to align  the fibre axes.  a clamping member (18) may be movably  mounted on the body (16) to clamp optical fibres (116)  of different diameters and may have a resilient arm (70)  engageable with the other body (16) to retain the exten­ sions (42) against the fibres (116).
