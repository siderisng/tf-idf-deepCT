<ABSTRACT>
a gear shift control system for an automatic transmission  comprises an air flow sensor (1) which senses the quantity of  air taken into an internal combustion engine and supplies a  corresponding electrical signal (q) to a differentiator (3) which  provides a derivative (q) of the sensed intake air quantity.  a  vehicle speed sensor (4) senses the speed of rotation of the  engine crankshaft to generate a corresponding signal (v).  a  gear shift decision circuit (22) is receptive of signals from the  air flow sensor (1) and the vehicle speed sensor (4) to define a  gear shift control line on a two-dimensional coordinate sysÂ­ tem as a function of the received input signals on respective  co-ordinate axes.  the derivative (q) of the sensed air quantity  (q) is used to shift the gear shift control line to a higher speed  side of the co-ordinate system to permit the transmission to  effect downshift operation.  
</ABSTRACT>
