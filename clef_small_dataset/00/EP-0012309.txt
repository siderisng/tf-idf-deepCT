<ABSTRACT>
It is proposed a fuel injection system for diesel combustion machines, in particular for vehicle diesel engines, through which the ignition conditions of a diesel motor even during operation in large heights remain optimally preserved despite reducing air filling. The system comprises a force of fabric injection pump operated with a speed-dependent injection timing adjusting device (spray adjuster 56) and a control device (58) operated depending on the amount of air supplied to the motor. Due to the control device (58), the at least speed controlled by the spray adjuster (56) is a pressure-dependent conveying change change and thus air-volume-dependent correction is stored. When using a hydraulic spray adjuster (56), the conveying pressure of a control pump (26) is increased by a correction actuator (62) of the control device (58) to reduce the amount of air in the sense of a pre-laying (early displacement) of the conveying start.
