process and apparatus for the removal of ammonium carbamate from a urea-synthesis solution.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
a process and apparatus for the removal of ammonium  carbamate from an aqueous urea solution wherein the  aqueous urea solution is introduced into a stripping zone and  caused to flow down a heat exchange wall as a thin film  while being heated and contacted in counter-current relation  with a gaseous stripping agent.  the aqueous urea solution,  while being stripped, is heated for at most 5 seconds at a  temperature in the range of from 180 - 195°c and thereafter  cooled to a temperature of from 155 - 170°c.  
</ABSTRACT>
