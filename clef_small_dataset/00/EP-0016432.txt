Preparation of 1-amino-2-phenoxy -4-hydroxyanthrobinone compounds by reacting 1-amino-2-chloro-4-hydroxy-anthraquinone in aqueous medium at pH 9 to 13 with the phenol at 120 to 150 ° C in the presence of phase transfer catalysts. as such the NEN a) ammonium salts of the formula (R) ₄ A˙, in the mimure, a R linear or branched C₄ to C₂₀ alkyl or C₇-C₁₀-phenyl-alkyl, the residual R C₁ to C₄-alkyl whereby one of the remaining R is also phenyl, or (R) ₃ pyridinium and a˙ an equivalent of an anion, b) ethylene oxide / propylene oxide block copolymers of α) hydro xyl compounds of formula A (OH) M in which A is a M- Valentic aliphatic residue with 2 to 6 C atoms, OH primary or secondary hyroxyl and M 2 to 4, with 2 to 40 mol of PO per equivalent OH and then with 2 to 40 moles per equiva lent oxpropylation product or β) amines of the Formula H₂N- (R-N / R¹) NH, in the R-saturated, linear or branched C₂ to C₆ alkylene, R¹ is hydrogen, methyl or phenyl, N 1 to 5, with 2 to 40 mole of PO per equivalent aminewater substance and then with 2 to 40 moles of ion per equivalent of oxpropylic product or c) oxethylation products of C₂ to C₂₀ alkylphenols, of C₁₂ - to C₂₀ alkenyl or alkylamines or from C₈ to C₂₀ alkanols with 3 to 30 moles of ion. In high yield, a very pure process product is obtained, which is suitable for all purposes.
