<ABSTRACT>
Method for generating a hot gas flow by combustion of preferably liquid fuels, on the basis of the following process steps: a) atomizing of the liquid fuel to fine particles; b) further atomization of the generated fuel particles with the aid of combustion air supplied as a sputtering medium and reducing the central fuel particles diameter to less than half of the mean diameter achieved at step a); c) burning the fuel-air mixture in a flame cone arranged around an axis A under stoichiometric conditions or at oxygen excess in an air guide tube; d) introducing a current to heating cold air or other medium, optionally with oxidizable proportions, in a plane E, which is substantially perpendicular to the axis A. The invention further relates to a hot gas generator or combustion furnace for carrying out the method with a straight air guide pipe in which coaxial a burner for liquid fuels with two-stage sputtering arrangement is arranged, with which a flame cone lying in the axis of the air guide tube is generated.
