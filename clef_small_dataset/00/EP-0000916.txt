<ABSTRACT>
There is described a process for the direct preparation of fatty acid adruments and glycerol from glycerides, in particular of natural fats and oils, in which the glycerides in the liquid phase with an ammonia current of at least 200 l / kg glyceride and hour at temperatures of 220 to 300 ° C in the presence of lead, zinc, cadmium, tin, titanium, zirconia, chromium, antimony, manganese, iron, nickel or cobalt salts of carboxylic acids or sulfonic acids are treated as catalysts and the emerging product mixture of a Phase separation is subjected to forming a fatty acid nitrile phase and a glycerol / water phase. The fatty acid nitrile phase is expediently subjected to aftertreatment in the presence of ammonia and said catalysts to completely transform intermediately formed fatty acid and fatty acid amide into nitrile. The raw glycerol can be cleaned by known methods. Fatty acid nitriles are known intermediates for the preparation of primary amines and quaternary ammonium compounds, which in turn are used in particular as textile and flotation aids. Glycerin is used primarily in the cosmetics, food industry, explosives manufacturing, as solvents and for many other purposes.
