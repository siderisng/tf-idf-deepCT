<ABSTRACT>
in a fire-resistant ceiling, non-flammable panels (18) are supported by spring strips (23) made of the same non-flammable material, which engage in the grooves of adjacent panels and have a greater width (b) than the mutually facing grooves (22) of the adjacent panels (18).  the springs have on their undersides (30) facing the room a coating (28) or covering (29) and serve simultaneously as supporting members and sound-absorbing members.  as a result of this arrangement, a grillage for the ceiling is not necessary.  the ceiling therefore has a low overall height and good sound-proofing.  <image>
