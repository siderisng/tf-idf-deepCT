<ABSTRACT>
a catalyst comprising an oxide complex of the general  formula   fe1-50bi0. 1-20sb1-100aabbccox   in which   a represents cu, ce, u, v, cr, mn and/or te;   b represents an alkali metal, alkaline earth metal, co, ni,  zn, cd, rare earth, ga, tl and/or th;   c represents mo, w, nb, ta, sn, ge, ti, zr, p, b, and/or  as;   a is 0 - 20;   b is 0 - 20;   c is 0 - 20;   a+b+c is greater than 0 and   x is a number determined by the oxygen requirements of  the other elements present.  the catalysts are useful in the production of nitriles by  the vapour phase ammoxidation of olefins, aldehydes and  alcohols; the vapour phase oxidation of olefins containing  not more than three contiguous carbon atoms and the  vapour phase oxydehydrogenation of olefins containing four  or more contiguous carbon atoms.  a particularly preferred catalyst according to the invention  has the formula w0. 2mo₁bi₂cu₃fe₁₂ox.
