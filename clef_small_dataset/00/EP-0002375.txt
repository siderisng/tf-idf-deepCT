<ABSTRACT>
thermistors comprising transition metal such as iron  rare earth of the lanthanide series or yttrium, and oxygen  (e. g.  prfeo₃) exhibit sufficient independence to variation in  oxygen partial pressure over a range of exhaust gas  conditions of internal combustion engines as to make them  particularly suitable for temperature compensation of oxygen  sensors such as those derived from titania as well as  temperature sensing in other oxygen varying environments.
