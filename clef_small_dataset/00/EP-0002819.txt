method for improving combustion processes.
</TITLE>

<ABSTRACT>
in a method for improving combustion processes, water vapour is fed to a mixture of air and hydrocarbon compounds in a combustion chamber prior to combustion in order to effect a considerable improvement in the progress of combustion, because, in contrast to air, water vapour has a substantially higher oxygen content.  catalysts, such as graphite and/or sponge iron, are used during combustion in order to promote the dissociation of the water into h2 + o2.  the method can be employed without special structural alterations in internal combustion engines, incineration plants and ovens.  <image>
