<ABSTRACT>
a battery charging system includes an alternator (10)  with a rectifier (12, 13) connecting the alternator output to the  battery (14).  a voltage rectifier (15) controls the current in the  alternator field winding (11) in accordance with the battery  voltage.  a transistor switch (23) in the voltage regulator conÂ­ trols the bias current to the output stage of the regulator and is  in turn controlled by a differential amplifier (34, 35) through a  diode pump (40, 41, 42, 44).  the differential amplifier (34, 35)  is connected to detect the presence of an a. c.  on one phase of  the alternator and causes the transistor to be switched off,  disabling the output stage of the regulator, whenever no a. c.   signal is present.
