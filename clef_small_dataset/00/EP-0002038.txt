<ABSTRACT>
The capillary vessel should serve to remove and retain blood that takes the fingertip or the earlobe of a patient.In order to remove the removal simple and clean and to be able to store the removed blood immediately in a closed vessel, the vessel is assigned a insert (1) which inserts with its cylindrical body (5) into the mouth (7) of the receiving vessel (2)becomes.On the cylindrical main parts (5) of the insert, a short, funnel-shaped part (4) connects, the tip (17) has such a small inner diameter, that the outside is sucked out by capillary action.The cylindrical body of the insert has such a large inner diameter that the blood is not inhibited by capillary action, but can flow free.
