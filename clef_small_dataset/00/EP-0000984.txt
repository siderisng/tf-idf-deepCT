<ABSTRACT>
an implanted receiver-decoder stores program bits  corresponding to rate alteration and to input amplifier sensitivity.   the former bits operate a rate decoder (4) which  in turn is responsive to predetermined outputs of a fixed  rate oscillatorcounter (1, 2).  the input amplifier (12) is  connected to the intermediate tap of a potential divider  (11, 14, 15), one arm (11) of which senses signals at the  heart, and the other of which is either set at a fixed potential,  or is allowed to float, under control of the input amplifier  sensitivity programming signal.
