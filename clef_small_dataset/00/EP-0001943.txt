<TITLE>
polycrystalline silicon shaped as a thin film for photovoltaic conversion.
</TITLE>
<APPLICANT>
rhone poulenc indfr<sep>rhone-poulenc industries<sep>rhone-poulenc industries22 avenue montaigne75008 parisfr <sep>
</APPLICANT>
<INVENTOR>
anglerot didier fr  <sep>anglerot, didier<sep>anglerot, didier17, rue labois-rouillonf-75019 - parisfr<sep>
</INVENTOR>
<ABSTRACT>
a process for the manufacture of polycrystalline silicon as a thin film, consisting in reducing a silicon compound in the gaseous state with a reducing metal in the liquid state, chosen from the group comprising aluminium, gallium and indium, the temperature of the reduction being between 700 and 800 deg c and the total pressure 1 atmosphere.  the silicon obtained by this process is applicable to photovoltaic conversion. 
</ABSTRACT>
