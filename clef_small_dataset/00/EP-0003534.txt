<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a process for the preparation of boron-containing phenol-formaldehyde resins by reacting diphenylolalkan novolaks with unsaturated fatty acids or fatty acid esters and boron compounds characterized in that diphenylolalkan novolac having a molecular weight 400 to 1600 with 15 to 35 wt.-TLN of an unsaturated fatty acid or their esters react under azeotropic drainage and then with 20 to 100 wt.-TLN of a boric acid trialkyl ester with 1 to 4 C atoms per alkyl chain or 14 to 72 wt.Parts of a boric acid alkyl ethrydride of the structure (RO) ₂B-O-B (OR) ₂ with 1 to 4 C atoms per alkyl chain R, or 10 to 50 wt.parts, preferably 15 to 20 wt.parts, boron trioxide or with 18 to 90 wt.parts, preferably 25 to 36 wt.-TLN, boric acid converts into the presence of a C₁-C₄ alcohol.
</ABSTRACT>
