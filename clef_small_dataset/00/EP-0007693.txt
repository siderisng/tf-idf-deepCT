<ABSTRACT>
black, flame-resistant, substantially inert, non-­ conductive, flexible textile materials are produced by impre­ gnating a scoured regenerated cellulosic textile material with  a composition containing (a) a compound having in one  tautomeric form, the general formula    in which x is oxygen, sulphur or -nh-, and 2 is -nh₂, -nhcn,  -nh-nh₂, -nh. co. nh₂ or lower alkyl or x and z taken together  represent a trivalent nitrogen atom, and (b) an ammonium  salt of an oxy-acid of phosphorus, heating the impregnated  textile in an oxygen-containing gas to a temperature of 220°-  300°c until a black, flame retardant product is obtained and  there has been a loss in weight based upon the original weight  of the textile material, of 20% to 30%.
