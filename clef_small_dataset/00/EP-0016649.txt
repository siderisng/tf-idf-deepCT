<ABSTRACT>
a reformed rice product is made by extruding a dough  through a low-pressure, pasta-type extruder to form simu­ lated rice grains which are then dried at a temperature of up  to 150°c to a final moisture content not exceeding 15% of the  product.  the dough is made by adding water to a dry  composition comprising a mixture of pregelatinised and  ungelatinised flour, sodium chloride and fat in powder form.
