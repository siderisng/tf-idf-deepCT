<ABSTRACT>
The invention relates to a continuously controllable gasification burner. The burner has a three-part housing (18,21,25). The housing (18,21,25) includes an annular channel (11) with a heat source (12). In the annular channel (11), fuel is evaporated, which flows through a front room (10) to a vortex (1) via a swirl panel (13). In the vortex (1) is mixed from an annular space (4) via radial channels (6) for heated primary air preheated at the heat source (B). The vapor-air mixture flows to a catalyst device (2) and is converted there into fuel gas, which is homogeneously mixed in a mixing chamber (3) with the aid of the swirl panel (24) with secondary air. In an ignition chamber (7), the ignition of the fuel gas air mixture takes place. The mixture enters blue-burning into a room (8). The annular space (4) surrounds concentrically the vortex (1), the catalyst device (2), the mixing chamber (3), the ignition chamber (7) and at least partially the space (8). In the annular space (4), the primary air is preheated by the heat to be extensive from the burner to the outside. The overall arrangement achieves extensive adjustability and reduces the pollutant content of the exhaust gases.
