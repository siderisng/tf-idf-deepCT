<ABSTRACT>
alkylation of aromatic hydrocarbons, especially toluene  methylation, is carried out in the presence of a catalyst  comprising an aluminosilicate zeolite of the zsm-5 type  in which some or all of the protons of the zeolite have  been replaced by monovalent cations.  preferably, the replacement  cations are sodium ions, the catalyst preferably  containing more than 2% by weight of sodium.
