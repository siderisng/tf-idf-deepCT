<ABSTRACT>
a tripod type constant velocity universal joint compri­ ses a spider member (10) having three roller-supporting  rods (11, 12, 13) which extend radially from a centre por­ tion of the spider member, with a respective roller element  (15, 16, 17) rotatably supported on and slidable along each  rod, and a pot member (18) defining three pairs of roller  raceways (19, 20, 21; 65).  the distances between the longitudinal axis of each  pair of roller raceways (19, 20, 21; 65) and the rotational  axis of the pot member at both ends (s, t) of the pair of  roller raceways are shorter than the distance therebetween  at a central portion (n) thereof, with both ends of each  pair of roller raceways being inwardly displaced or curved.   this universal joint reduces the quantities of non-constant  rotational velocity by reducing the differences in the pitch  radii of the portions at which two adjacent roller elements  are respectively disposed between the pairs of roller race­ ways, and reduces the size and weight of the joint.  
</ABSTRACT>
