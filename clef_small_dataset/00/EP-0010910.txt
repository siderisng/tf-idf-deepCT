<ABSTRACT>
an insulating film layer of silicon oxynitride is formed  on a silicon semiconductor substrate surface by contacting  the surface of the silicon substrate at an elevated tempera­ ture with an ammonia gas atmosphere containing an oxy­ gen-containing substance gas, for example, oxygen gas,  carbon dioxide gas, carbon monoxide gas, nitrogen mono­ xide gas, nitrogen dioxide gas, or water vapor, in a volume  concentration in terms of molecular oxygen of above 100  ppm up to 10 000 ppm.
