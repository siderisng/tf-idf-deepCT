<ABSTRACT>
A self-curing mass based on polymum thacrylates is made of a fine-pitweed, solid polymer phase of polymethyl methacrylate and / or poly-2-hydroxyethyl methacrylate and a liquid monomer phase of methyl methacrylate and / or 2-hydroxyethyl methacrylate, wherein the weight ratio of polymer phase to monomer phase 1 , 5 to 3.3: 1 and the proportion of poly-2 hydroxyethyl methacrylate in the cured product at least 2 wt. % and at most 99 wt. % is. Further usual additives, in particular initiators, such as hydroperoxides and peroxides, activators, such as alkyl, alkylaryl and oxalkylamines, and inhibitors, X-ray contrast agents and the like, may be admixed to one or both phases. Further, a method for producing the self-curing mass is proposed. The mass can be particularly advantageously used as bone cement in surgery and orthopedics, as it adheres to both the prosthesis and the cement bone bone and has a pronounced swelling power which acts stimulating on the bone wax and thereby a desired reinforcement the kno chencing can lead.
