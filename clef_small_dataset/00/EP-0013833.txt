<ABSTRACT>
a liquid polymer is formed in a low temperature polymeri­ zation reaction involving organo-metallic sodium and alumi­ nium compounds, at least one of which is partially hydrolyzed.   the polymer is hydrolyzed, dried to form an amorphous sodi­ um beta-alumina precursor, and then heated, at between  1,200°c and about 1,550°c, to form a ceramic, comprising  ion-conductive sodium beta-alumina which is useful as a solid  electrolyte.
