<ABSTRACT>
the garage is only slightly larger than a motor car.  the problem to be solved is that is should be possible to drive in and out in the same way as when parking a car at the kerb, no more space being necessary in addition to the garage than when parking on the street.  the solution to the object is a small garage which has a wall (1) as a supporting structure for its roof (2) on only one longitudinal side, having a single door (3) on the other three sides, which closes three sides and can be raised.  at both ends of the garage there is an upper swivel arm (4) and a lower swivel arm (5), the lower swivel arms having a bend which keeps the door opening free when the door is open.  at the inner side of the supporting structure, there is a kerb (6).  the principal application is in situations where there is insufficient space available for the erection of a conventional garage.  <image>
</ABSTRACT>
